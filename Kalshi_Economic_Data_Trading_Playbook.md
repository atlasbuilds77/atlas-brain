# Kalshi Economic Data Trading Playbook
## Comprehensive Guide to Scalping Economic Data Markets

### Executive Summary
Kalshi is a regulated prediction market exchange that allows direct trading on economic data outcomes. Unlike traditional markets where you trade instruments affected by data, Kalshi lets you trade directly on whether specific economic thresholds will be met. This playbook outlines strategies for scalping economic data releases on Kalshi, focusing on high-impact events like NFP, CPI, GDP, Fed decisions, and other key indicators.

### Market Structure & Mechanics
- **Binary Contracts**: Each contract pays $1.00 if the event occurs, $0.00 if it doesn't
- **Price as Probability**: Contract price represents market-implied probability (e.g., $0.74 = 74% chance)
- **Settlement**: Based on official government releases (BLS for employment/CPI, Fed for rates, BEA for GDP)
- **Liquidity**: $23.8B-$40B notional volume in 2025, growing 1,200% YoY
- **Regulation**: CFTC-regulated, same legal standing as CME

## 1. NON-FARM PAYROLLS (NFP) / JOBS REPORT

### Kalshi Markets Available
- "Will NFP be above/below X thousand?"
- "Will unemployment rate be above/below X%?"
- "Will average hourly earnings increase by X%?"
- Employment combo markets (U3 + NFP)

### Trading Patterns
**Pre-Announcement (Days Before):**
- Prices reflect consensus forecasts from economists
- Volatility increases as release approaches
- Market makers widen spreads 1-2 hours before release
- Look for discrepancies between related markets (e.g., NFP vs. unemployment rate)

**Post-Announcement (First 5-15 Minutes):**
- Immediate price adjustment to actual data
- Large spreads initially, then rapid convergence
- Opportunities for quick scalps as market digests surprise magnitude
- Typical movement: 20-40¢ price swings on major surprises

**Scalping Strategy:**
1. **Pre-release positioning**: Enter 30-60 minutes before announcement with small position
2. **Directional bias**: Based on ADP report, jobless claims, and other leading indicators
3. **Post-release scalping**: Enter immediately after data release during initial volatility
4. **Exit**: Within 5-15 minutes, don't hold through full market digestion

**Key Levels to Watch:**
- Consensus estimate (most important)
- Previous month's reading
- 3-month average
- 100K+ surprises create largest moves

## 2. CONSUMER PRICE INDEX (CPI) / INFLATION

### Kalshi Markets Available
- "Will headline CPI be above/below X% YoY?"
- "Will core CPI be above/below X% YoY?"
- Monthly and quarterly inflation markets
- Fed reaction markets (linked to CPI outcomes)

### Trading Patterns
**Pre-Announcement:**
- Market often anticipates "sticky" inflation weeks before release
- Energy prices, shipping data, and commodity prices provide leading indicators
- Prices become more volatile in final 24 hours
- Watch for discrepancies between headline and core CPI expectations

**Post-Announcement:**
- Immediate repricing based on surprise magnitude
- Larger moves when data contradicts Fed narrative
- Secondary moves as market interprets Fed implications
- Core CPI typically more important than headline

**Scalping Strategy:**
1. **Pre-positioning**: Based on real-time inflation indicators (gas prices, rent data)
2. **Straddle positions**: Consider both sides if uncertainty is high
3. **Quick exits**: Scalp the initial 2-5 minute move
4. **Follow-through**: Watch for secondary moves as analysts adjust forecasts

**Critical Thresholds:**
- Fed's 2% target (psychological level)
- 0.1-0.2% surprises trigger significant moves
- Sequential changes (acceleration/deceleration) matter

## 3. GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT (GDP) RELEASES

### Kalshi Markets Available
- "Will QX GDP growth be above/below X%?"
- Advance, preliminary, and final estimate markets
- Real vs. nominal GDP markets

### Trading Patterns
**Pre-Announcement:**
- Less volatile than CPI/NFP but still significant
- Market incorporates leading indicators (ISM, retail sales, housing)
- Revisions markets can be particularly interesting

**Post-Announcement:**
- Slower digestion than employment/inflation data
- Multiple revisions create ongoing opportunities
- Watch for growth vs. recession threshold markets

**Scalping Strategy:**
1. **Focus on surprises**: GDP revisions often provide cleaner moves
2. **Quarter-over-quarter vs. year-over-year**: Understand which metric the market cares about
3. **Economic cycle positioning**: Recession probability markets during uncertain times

## 4. FEDERAL RESERVE RATE DECISIONS

### Kalshi Markets Available
- "Will Fed raise/cut/hold rates at X meeting?"
- Specific basis point change markets
- Forward guidance markets
- Dot plot interpretation markets

### Trading Patterns
**Pre-Announcement:**
- Prices reflect Fed funds futures plus risk premium
- Volatility increases with Fed speaker commentary
- Last-minute positioning before blackout period
- Watch for discrepancies between meeting outcome and forward guidance

**Post-Announcement:**
- Immediate settlement for rate decision
- Continued trading on forward guidance implications
- Press conference creates secondary volatility
- Dot plot releases (quarterly) create extended trading windows

**Scalping Strategy:**
1. **Pre-meeting positioning**: Based on economic data since last meeting
2. **Statement vs. press conference**: Trade the initial reaction, then Powell's comments
3. **Forward guidance focus**: Markets often care more about future path than current decision
4. **Dot plot trading**: Quarterly opportunities with multiple data points

## 5. OTHER ECONOMIC INDICATORS

### Housing Data
- **Markets**: Housing starts, building permits, existing home sales
- **Patterns**: Seasonal adjustments important, weather effects
- **Strategy**: Focus on trend changes rather than single data points

### Retail Sales
- **Markets**: Monthly retail sales growth
- **Patterns**: Holiday season effects, e-commerce vs. brick-and-mortar
- **Strategy**: Watch consumer sentiment as leading indicator

### Manufacturing Data
- **Markets**: ISM PMI, industrial production
- **Patterns**: Expansion/contraction thresholds (50 level for PMI)
- **Strategy**: Regional Fed surveys as leading indicators

## SPECIFIC TRADE SETUPS

### Setup 1: Pre-Announcement Consensus Play
**Scenario**: Strong consensus with lopsided positioning
**Entry**: 1-2 hours before release
**Position**: Contrary to consensus if risk/reward favorable
**Exit**: Immediately after data release
**Risk**: High if consensus is correct
**Reward**: High if surprise occurs

### Setup 2: Post-Release Volatility Scalp
**Scenario**: Major data surprise
**Entry**: Within 30 seconds of data release
**Position**: Direction of initial move
**Exit**: Within 2-5 minutes
**Risk**: Medium (quick reversal risk)
**Reward**: Medium-high

### Setup 3: Straddle/Strangle for High Uncertainty
**Scenario**: High-impact event with divided expectations
**Entry**: 30 minutes before release
**Position**: Both sides of key threshold
**Exit**: After volatility subsides
**Risk**: Limited to premium paid
**Reward**: Captures large move in either direction

### Setup 4: Correlation Arbitrage
**Scenario**: Related markets mispriced relative to each other
**Example**: NFP strong but unemployment rate doesn't adjust
**Entry**: When discrepancy identified
**Position**: Long undervalued, short overvalued
**Exit**: When correlation re-establishes
**Risk**: Low-medium
**Reward**: Medium

## RISK MANAGEMENT

### Position Sizing
- Never risk more than 1-2% of capital on single trade
- Scale into positions before major events
- Use stop-loss orders for directional trades

### Volatility Considerations
- Spreads widen significantly pre-announcement
- Liquidity can dry up momentarily post-release
- Have exit strategy before entering

### Market Regime Awareness
- **Risk-On**: Growth data more important
- **Risk-Off**: Inflation/Fed data dominates
- **Transition Periods**: Increased cross-market correlations

### Technical Factors
- Monitor trading volume trends
- Watch bid-ask spreads for liquidity signals
- Be aware of maximum position limits

## PSYCHOLOGICAL DISCIPLINE

### Common Pitfalls
1. **Overtrading**: Too many positions around events
2. **Confirmation Bias**: Ignoring contrary signals
3. **Revenge Trading**: Trying to recover losses immediately
4. **Anchoring**: Sticking to initial view despite new data

### Best Practices
1. **Pre-defined Plans**: Enter with clear entry/exit rules
2. **Post-Trade Review**: Analyze what worked/didn't
3. **Emotional Control**: Recognize when to step away
4. **Continuous Learning**: Adapt strategies as markets evolve
