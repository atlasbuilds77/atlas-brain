# SPX Day Trading Guide: Market Structure & Technical Analysis

## Executive Summary
This guide provides actionable insights for day trading the S&P 500 Index (SPX), focusing on market structure, technical analysis, and intraday dynamics. The SPX represents the 500 largest U.S. companies and serves as the primary benchmark for U.S. equity markets.

## 1. Key Support & Resistance Levels

### Identifying Levels:
- **Pivot Points**: Calculate using previous day's high, low, and close
  - Pivot = (H + L + C) / 3
  - R1 = (2 × Pivot) - L
  - S1 = (2 × Pivot) - H
  - R2 = Pivot + (H - L)
  - S2 = Pivot - (H - L)

- **Gamma Levels**: Monitor dealer positioning via options data
  - Call Wall: Typically acts as resistance (holds 83% of sessions)
  - Put Wall: Typically acts as support
  - SpotGamma data shows SPX closes below Call Wall 88% of sessions

- **Previous Day Levels**:
  - Previous day high/low
  - Previous day close (gap reference)
  - Pre-market high/low

- **Round Numbers**: Psychological levels (e.g., 6800, 6900, 7000)

### Actionable Approach:
1. Mark key levels before market open
2. Watch for reactions at these levels (rejection or breakthrough)
3. Use confluence of multiple levels for higher probability trades

## 2. Common Intraday Patterns

### Opening Range Breakout (ORB):
- **Definition**: First 15-30 minutes establishes range
- **Statistics**: 64.41% of upside breakouts lead to green closes
- **Strongest Days**: Tuesday, Thursday, Friday (>70% success)
- **Entry**: Break above/below opening range with volume
- **Stop**: 75% retracement of opening range (only 15% probability of hitting)

### Gap Fills:
- **Overall Fill Rate**: ~60% during uptrends
- **Day-by-Day Statistics**:
  - Monday: Gap down fills 70%, Gap up fills 54%
  - Tuesday: Gap down fills 55%, Gap up fills 67%
  - Wednesday: Gap down fills 80%, Gap up fills 62%
  - Thursday: Gap down fills 60%, Gap up fills 57%
  - Friday: Gap down fills 20%, Gap up fills 64%

- **Gap Size Matters**:
  - 0-0.19% gaps: 87-92% fill rate
  - 0.2-0.39% gaps: 53-69% fill rate
  - >0.4% gaps: Below 50% fill rate

- **Time Factor**: 80%+ of gaps fill by noon EST

### Reversal Patterns:
- **Double Tops/Bottoms**: At key resistance/support
- **Bull/Bear Flags**: Continuation patterns
- **TCs (Trend Continuation)**: Pullback to support/resistance
- **RBs (Reversal Bars)**: Key reversal candles at extremes

## 3. Volume Profile & VWAP Usage

### Volume Profile:
- **Value Area**: Where 70% of volume traded
- **Point of Control**: Price with highest volume
- **Volume Gaps**: Areas with minimal volume (breakout zones)

### VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price):
- **Primary Use**: Intraday trend validation
- **Key Concepts**:
  - Price above VWAP = bullish bias
  - Price below VWAP = bearish bias
  - Virgin VWAP: First touch of VWAP from above/below
  - Anchored VWAP: From significant events (gaps, news)

- **Trading Strategies**:
  1. **VWAP Pullback**: Enter near VWAP with trend
  2. **VWAP Rejection**: Fade moves away from VWAP
  3. **VWAP Break**: Trade breakouts with volume confirmation

- **Confluence**: Combine with:
  - 50-EMA for trend filter
  - RSI for overbought/oversold
  - Bollinger Bands for volatility

## 4. Key Times of Day for Volatility

### Market Session Structure:
1. **Pre-Market (4:00-9:30 AM EST)**: News reaction, gap formation
2. **Opening Bell (9:30 AM)**: Highest volatility, establish range
3. **10:30 AM Reversal**: Common reversal time (institutional activity)
4. **Midday (11:00 AM - 2:30 PM)**: Lower volatility, range-bound often
5. **Power Hour (3:00-4:00 PM)**: Institutional rebalancing, volume surge
6. **Close (4:00 PM)**: Final positioning, MOC (Market on Close) orders

### Actionable Times:
- **9:30-10:30 AM**: Opening range establishment
- **10:30 AM**: Watch for reversal signals
- **3:00-4:00 PM**: Momentum moves, trend acceleration
- **3:50-4:00 PM**: Final squeeze attempts

## 5. Reading SPX Order Flow & Institutional Activity

### Order Flow Components:
1. **Time & Sales (Tape Reading)**:
   - Large prints (>100 contracts) = institutional activity
   - Sequence of prints at bid/ask shows direction
   - Absorption: Large offers/bids being taken

2. **Level 2 Data**:
   - Bid/ask size changes
   - Iceberg orders (hidden liquidity)
   - Order book imbalance

3. **Options Flow**:
   - Large SPX option trades indicate institutional positioning
   - Unusual activity: Volume spikes vs. open interest
   - Call/Put ratios for sentiment

### Institutional Patterns:
- **Morning Flow**: Establish positions
- **Midday Adjustments**: Hedge/scale positions
- **Power Hour**: Final positioning
- **MOC Orders**: 3:50-4:00 PM execution

### Actionable Signals:
- **Absorption at Support/Resistance**: Institutional buying/selling
- **Large Print Sequences**: Follow the flow
- **Options Activity**: Gamma positioning clues

## 6. VIX Relationship to SPX

### Core Relationship:
- **80% Inverse Correlation**: VIX rises when SPX falls
- **20% Same Direction**: During panic or euphoria
- **VIX Calculation**: Based on SPX options midpoint quotes

### Using VIX for Timing:
1. **VIX Spikes**: Often coincide with market bottoms
2. **VIX Compression**: Low VIX during rallies (complacency)
3. **VIX Divergence**: SPX makes new high/low but VIX doesn't confirm

### VIX Levels Interpretation:
- **VIX < 15**: Low volatility, complacent market
- **VIX 15-25**: Normal range
- **VIX > 25**: Elevated fear, higher volatility expected
- **VIX > 40**: Extreme fear, potential reversal

### Expected Moves:
- Daily expected move = VIX / √365
- Example: VIX at 20 = ~1.05% daily expected move
- Weekly expected move = VIX / √52

## 7. Gamma Squeeze Levels & Dealer Positioning

### Gamma Exposure (GEX):
- **Positive Gamma**: Dealers are long options, stabilize market
- **Negative Gamma**: Dealers are short options, amplify moves

### Key Levels:
- **Call Wall**: Largest call OI, acts as resistance
- **Put Wall**: Largest put OI, acts as support
- **Gamma Flip**: Price where gamma changes sign

### Dealer Behavior:
- **Long Gamma**: Buy dips, sell rallies (range-bound)
- **Short Gamma**: Sell dips, buy rallies (trending)
- **Vanna Effect**: Delta changes with volatility shifts

### Actionable Setup:
1. **Identify Gamma Levels**: Via SpotGamma or similar
2. **Watch VIX Trend**: Falling VIX + rising SPX = mechanical support
3. **Breakthrough Setup**: Break through gamma level with falling vol
4. **Risk**: Squeezes can reverse quickly

## 8. Major Moving Averages

### Key Averages:
1. **20-Day MA**: Short-term trend, often acts as support/resistance
2. **50-Day MA**: Intermediate trend, watched by institutions
3. **200-Day MA**: Long-term trend, "bull/bear market" divider

### Trading Significance:
- **Price > All MAs**: Bullish trend
- **Price < All MAs**: Bearish trend
- **MA Crossovers**: Golden Cross (50 > 200), Death Cross (50 < 200)
- **Pullbacks to MAs**: Common entry points in trends

### Intraday Application:
- Use shorter MAs (9, 20) on intraday charts
- Watch for reactions at daily MA levels
- Combine with volume for confirmation

## 9. News & Macro Events Impact

### Scheduled Events:
1. **Economic Data**:
   - CPI/PCE (8:30 AM): Immediate volatility
   - Jobs Report (8:30 AM): Gap and trend
   - FOMC (2:00 PM): Afternoon volatility
   - GDP (8:30 AM): Directional moves

2. **Earnings Season**:
   - Heavy before/after hours
   - Index reaction to major components
   - Sector rotation implications

### Trading Around Events:
- **Pre-Event**: Reduced positioning, volatility compression
- **Immediate Reaction**: Often exaggerated, may reverse
- **Post-Event**: Trend establishment, follow-through

### Actionable Approach:
1. **Reduce Size**: Before major events
2. **Wait for Reaction**: Don't pre-position
3. **Trade the Range**: Initial volatility often mean-reverts
4. **Follow Institutional Flow**: Watch tape for direction

## Daily Trading Routine

### Pre-Market (8:00-9:30 AM):
1. Review overnight news/globals
2. Check pre-market levels vs. previous close
3. Identify key support/resistance
4. Review options positioning (gamma levels)
5. Plan gap fill probabilities

### Market Open (9:30-10:30 AM):
1. Establish opening range
2. Watch for ORB setup
3. Monitor initial volume
4. Identify early trend

### Morning Session (10:30 AM - 12:00 PM):
1. Watch for 10:30 reversal
2. Trade gap fills if applicable
3. Monitor VWAP relationship
4. Watch institutional flow

### Afternoon Session (1:00-3:00 PM):
1. Lower activity period

3. Prepare for power hour
4. Watch for breakout setups

### Power Hour (3:00-4:00 PM):
1. Increased volume

3. Watch for squeeze attempts
4. Manage existing positions

2. Update statistics
3. Plan for next day
4. Monitor after-hours news

5. Keep statistics updated
