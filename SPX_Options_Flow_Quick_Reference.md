# SPX Options Flow: Quick Reference Guide

## Key Concepts at a Glance

### 1. Unusual Options Activity (UOA) Flags
- **Volume**: 5-10x average daily volume
- **Size**: 1,000+ contract transactions
- **Timing**: Anticipatory (1-3 days before moves)
- **SPX Specific**: More reliable than ETFs, European-style exercise

### 2. Call/Put Sweep Interpretation
| Type | Directional Bet | Hedging Activity |
|------|----------------|------------------|
| **Size** | 5,000+ contracts | Matches portfolio size |
| **Structure** | Single-leg, OTM strikes | Multi-leg, spreads |
| **Execution** | Aggressive fills | Systematic, across strikes |
| **Confirmation** | Follow-up equity flow | Portfolio rebalancing |

### 3. Gamma Positioning Impact
- **Positive Gamma**: Dealers long options → Stabilizes markets (buy dips, sell rallies)
- **Negative Gamma**: Dealers short options → Amplifies volatility (sell declines, buy rallies)
- **Key Levels**: Call Wall (resistance), Put Wall (support), Volatility Trigger
- **0DTE Reality**: >50% of SPX volume, creates intraday gamma explosions

### 4. Max Pain Practical Use
- **When it matters**: 1-2 days before expiration, low volatility environments
- **SPX advantage**: Stronger effects due to institutional concentration
- **Trading edge**: 78% close within 1-day gamma range
- **Combination**: Use with gamma levels for confluence

### 5. Smart Money vs Retail
**Smart Money:**
- Large size (1,000+ contracts)
- Complex strategies
- Anticipatory timing
- Dark pool confirmation

**Retail Flow:**
- Small size (<100 contracts)
- Simple calls/puts
- Reactive to news
- Sentiment extremes

### 6. Dark Pool Confirmation
- **Minimum**: 10,000+ share blocks
- **SPX proxies**: SPY/ES block trades
- **Workflow**: Options flow → Dark pool → Futures verification
- **Timing**: Concurrent or preceding options activity

### 7. Put/Call Ratio Guide
| Ratio Level | Interpretation | SPX Context |
|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| **>1.5** | Extreme bearish (contrarian bullish) | Often hedging-related |
| **1.2-1.6** | Normal SPX range | Institutional protection |
| **0.8-1.2** | Neutral | Balanced sentiment |
| **<0.7** | Extreme bullish (contrarian bearish) | Rare for SPX |

### 8. Institutional Hedging Patterns
**Common Strategies:**
- Put spreads (cost-effective protection)
- Collars (call sales finance puts)
- Variance swaps (pure volatility)
- Dynamic delta hedging

**Cyclical Patterns:**
- Monthly/quarterly rebalancing
- Earnings season increases
- Macro event preparation
- Window dressing periods

### 9. Window Dressing Calendar
**Month-End (Last 3 Days):**
- SPX outperformance
- Buying winners, selling losers
- Reversal first 3 days new month

**Quarter-End (More Pronounced):**
- Stronger rebalancing flows
- Performance reporting pressure
- Tax considerations

**Year-End (Most Significant):**
- Tax-loss harvesting
- Bonus period positioning
- Strategic repositioning

## Risk Management Rules

1. **Never trade on single data point** - Require multiple confirmations
2. **Size based on gamma environment** - Reduce in negative gamma
3. **Stops at gamma levels** - Not just price levels
4. **Maximum position limits** - Based on account risk parameters
5. **Regular stress testing** - Scenario analysis for extreme moves

## Critical Warning Signs

**Immediate Caution:**
- Extreme negative gamma readings
- 0DTE volume spikes during stress
- Put/call ratio >1.8 or <0.5
- Options flow without dark pool confirmation

**Medium-Term Concerns:**
- Sustained dealer short gamma
- Max pain far from current price approaching expiry
- Window dressing period reversals
- Correlation breakdowns in hedging
