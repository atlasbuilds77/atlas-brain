## Key Findings

### Kalshi Market Mechanics
- Binary contracts paying $1.00 if event occurs, $0.00 if not
- Price represents market-implied probability (e.g., $0.74 = 74% chance)
- Settlement based on official government data releases
- CFTC-regulated with $23.8B-$40B notional volume in 2025 (1,200% YoY growth)

### Economic Data Trading Patterns

#### 1. NFP/Jobs Report
- **Pre-announcement**: Prices reflect consensus, volatility increases, spreads widen
- **Post-announcement**: Immediate price adjustment, 20-40¢ swings on surprises
- **Scalping**: Enter within 30 seconds of release, exit within 5-15 minutes
- **Key levels**: ±100K from consensus creates largest moves

#### 2. CPI/Inflation
- **Pre-announcement**: Market anticipates "sticky" inflation weeks ahead
- **Post-announcement**: Core CPI more important than headline
- **Scalping**: Initial 2-5 minute moves most predictable
- **Critical thresholds**: Fed's 2% target, 0.1-0.2% surprises significant

#### 3. GDP Releases
- Less volatile than CPI/NFP but still significant
- Revisions markets provide cleaner opportunities
- Focus on growth vs. recession thresholds

#### 4. Fed Rate Decisions
- **Pre-meeting**: Prices reflect Fed funds futures plus risk premium
- **Post-announcement**: Immediate settlement for decision, continued trading on guidance
- **Scalping**: Trade initial statement reaction (2:00 PM) and press conference (2:30 PM)
- Forward guidance often more important than current decision

#### 5. Other Indicators
- Housing data: Seasonal adjustments important
- Retail sales: Consumer sentiment as leading indicator
- Manufacturing: Expansion/contraction thresholds (50 PMI level)
