- **Good Deal:** $250-300

**2. Creality Ender 3 V2 / V3 SE**
- **Why:** Most common on used market, cheap, massive community
- **Used Price:** $80-150
- **Pros:**
  - Very cheap
  - Huge modding community
  - Spare parts everywhere
  - Easy to learn on

- **Cons:**
  - Needs frequent tinkering/calibration
  - Manual bed leveling (upgradable)

  - "You'll upgrade everything eventually"
- **Good Deal:** $80-100
- **Fair Deal:** $100-150
- **Pass:** Over $150 (just buy new for $180)

### TIER 2: GREAT IF PRICED RIGHT

**3. Bambu Lab P1S / A1 Mini**
- **Why:** Fast, reliable, modern CoreXY
- **Used Price:** $400-600
- **Pros:**
  - Super fast prints (saves time)
  - Very reliable
  - Auto everything
  - Great community
- **Cons:**
  - Newer (less long-term reliability data)
  - More expensive
  - Proprietary ecosystem
- **Good Deal:** $400-500
- **Fair Deal:** $500-600
- **Pass:** Over $600 (too close to new price)

**4. Creality K1 / K1C**
- **Why:** Fast CoreXY, budget option
- **Used Price:** $250-350
- **Pros:**
  - Very fast
  - Enclosed (good for ABS)
  - Decent quality
- **Cons:**
  - Quality control issues when new
  - Mixed reliability reports
- **Good Deal:** $200-250
- **Fair Deal:** $250-350
- **Pass:** Over $350

### TIER 3: AVOID (For Your Use Case)

**Resin Printers (Elegoo Mars, Anycubic Photon, etc.)**
- **Why avoid:** 
  - Too small for robot parts
  - Messy (liquid resin)
  - Toxic fumes
  - Not practical for structural parts
  - Good for miniatures, bad for Atlas

**Older Prusa Models (MK2, MK2.5)**
- **Why avoid:**
  - Old firmware
  - Parts wearing out
  - Harder to find support
  - Only worth it if <$150

## ✅ WHAT TO CHECK BEFORE BUYING

### CRITICAL CHECKS (Must Do)

1. **Ask for live demo print**
   - Seller should print something in front of you
   - Watch first layer adhesion
   - Listen for grinding/skipping sounds
   - If they refuse → walk away

2. **Inspect the bed**
   - Look for warping (place ruler across it)
   - Check for scratches/gouges
   - PEI sheet condition (magnetic beds)
   - Glass beds: look for chips

3. **Check nozzle/hotend**
   - Should be clean (not caked in plastic)
   - Ask when last replaced
   - Brass nozzles wear out (~500 hours)

4. **Test all axes movement**
   - Manually move X, Y, Z by hand
   - Should be smooth, no grinding
   - Check belt tension (not too loose/tight)

5. **Ask about usage**
   - How many hours/prints?
   - What materials used? (ABS wears parts faster)
   - Any upgrades? (can be good or bad)
   - Why selling? (red flag if "just needs calibration")

6. **Verify accessories included**
   - Power supply
   - SD card / USB drive
   - Sample filament
   - Tools (hex keys, scraper)
   - Spare nozzles
   - Manual

### GOOD SIGNS

✅ Seller has multiple successful prints to show  
✅ Clean printer (shows care)  
✅ Original box/manuals  
✅ Upgraded parts (quality upgrades like metal extruder)  
✅ Seller knows specs/settings  
✅ Willing to demo print  
✅ Has spare parts included  
✅ Receipts/proof of purchase  
✅ Active Facebook profile (not new account)  

### RED FLAGS

❌ "Just needs calibration" or "just needs new nozzle"  
❌ Won't demo print ("no time" / "no filament")  
❌ Price too good to be true (<$50 for Ender 3)  
❌ Seller account created recently  
❌ Won't meet in person / wants shipping payment first  
❌ Caked in old plastic / visibly abused  
❌ Missing critical parts  
❌ "Selling for a friend"  
❌ Pushy about quick sale  
❌ Modified beyond recognition (too many mods = unstable)

## 🔧 FIRST UPGRADES TO BUY (If Getting Ender 3)

**Don't upgrade immediately** - learn the printer first

**When you do upgrade (priority order):**
1. **BLTouch / CRTouch** ($30-40) - Auto bed leveling, worth it
2. **Metal extruder** ($15-20) - Plastic one breaks
3. **PEI spring steel bed** ($20-30) - Easier part removal
4. **Capricorn tubing** ($10) - Better filament feeding
5. **Yellow bed springs** ($8) - Stiffer, holds level longer

**Total upgrade cost: ~$100** (still cheaper than Prusa)

## 🤖 FOR ATLAS BUILD SPECIFICALLY

**Print Bed Size Needed:**
- Minimum: 200mm × 200mm (most parts fit)
- Ideal: 220mm × 220mm or larger
- Atlas parts: Largest is 200mm × 150mm (chassis)

**All recommended printers meet this requirement**

**Filament for Atlas:**
- PLA+ (stronger than PLA, easy to print)
- All printers handle PLA+ fine
- 1kg roll = ~$25
- You'll need 2-3 rolls for full Atlas build

## 🚩 SCAM AWARENESS

**Common Facebook Marketplace scams:**

1. **"Shipped only" scams**
   - Sends fake tracking
   - Never ships item
   - **Solution:** Meet in person ONLY

2. **"Too good to be true" pricing**
   - Bambu Lab for $100? Scam.
   - **Solution:** Know fair market prices

3. **Fake profiles**
   - New account, no history
   - Stock photos only
   - **Solution:** Check profile age/activity

4. **Bait and switch**
   - Shows nice printer, brings broken one
   - **Solution:** Verify serial number in photos
