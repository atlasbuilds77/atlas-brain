# ICT Trading Concepts - Basics

## What is ICT?
Inner Circle Trader (ICT) methodology by Michael J. Huddleston. Focuses on understanding how institutions trade to predict price movements.

## Core Concepts

### 1. Liquidity Pools
WHERE retail traders put their stop losses. Smart money hunts these.

**Buy-side Liquidity (BSL):**
- Found ABOVE swing highs
- Where buy stop orders cluster
- Smart money sells into this liquidity

**Sell-side Liquidity (SSL):**
- Found BELOW swing lows
- Where sell stop orders cluster
- Smart money buys from this liquidity

**Key insight:** Traditional "support" and "resistance" are often just liquidity pools waiting to be raided.

### 2. Order Blocks
Areas where institutions entered positions. Often act as future support/resistance.

**Bullish Order Block:**
- Last DOWN candle before a strong up move
- Price often returns here to "retest" before continuing up

**Bearish Order Block:**
- Last UP candle before a strong down move
- Price often returns here before continuing down

### 3. Market Structure
**Break of Structure (BOS):** 
- Confirms trend continuation
- Higher high in uptrend, lower low in downtrend

**Change of Character (CHoCH):**
- Signals potential reversal
- First lower high in uptrend = warning sign

### 4. Fair Value Gaps (FVG)
Imbalances on the chart where price moved too fast, leaving a "gap" in fair value.
- Price often returns to fill these gaps
- Institutions use FVGs as entry points

### 5. Liquidity Sweeps
Price briefly breaks a level (triggering stops) then reverses sharply.
- This is institutions grabbing liquidity before the real move
- Don't chase the initial break - wait for reversal confirmation

## How to Apply
1. Identify market structure (bullish or bearish)
2. Find liquidity pools (where are stops clustered?)
3. Look for order blocks as potential entry zones
4. Watch for liquidity sweeps as entry signals
5. Target the opposite liquidity pool for exits
