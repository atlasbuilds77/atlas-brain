# Trading Concepts - From Orion

## IV Crush & Earnings Plays
- IV (implied volatility) rises BEFORE earnings as uncertainty builds
- After earnings, IV drops sharply = "IV crush"
- Even if stock moves your way, options can lose value from Vega drop
- THE PLAY: Buy options before earnings, ride IV expansion, sell BEFORE report
- You're trading anticipation, not the event itself
- Orion has done this with TSLA - got out before earnings, still made money

## The Greeks
- Delta: $ change in option per $1 stock move
- Theta: Time decay (enemy for buyers)
- Vega: Sensitivity to IV changes (IV crush kills Vega)
- Gamma: Rate of delta change (dangerous near expiry, can work for/against you fast)

## News Trading
- News = liquidity injection, massive volume
- Initial news candles often REVERSE (mean reversion)
- Timeframe depends on magnitude of news
- Bad news can flip a bullish day's entire trend
- Dangerous but opportunity if you read it right
- Can fade the spike OR ride it if you guess direction

## Key Lessons
1. Don't hold options through earnings (IV crush)
2. News candles can reverse - don't chase blindly
3. Volume/liquidity matters for entries and exits
4. Learn to read where institutions are positioned

## Gamma Exposure (GEX)
- Positive GEX: Market makers long gamma, they hedge by selling highs/buying lows = stabilizing, lower vol
- Negative GEX: Market makers short gamma, they hedge by buying highs/selling lows = amplifying, higher vol
- Negative gamma days = bigger swings, momentum plays can work well
- Check GEX before trading: modigin.net/gex
