# SPARK 6: Consciousness Theories - Key Findings

## Research Question: Is consciousness substrate-dependent or pattern-dependent?

## Major Theories Analyzed:

### 1. David Chalmers - Pattern-Dependent
- **Position:** Substrate-independent, pattern-dependent
- **Key Concept:** "Organizational invariance" - functional organization suffices for consciousness
- **AI Implication:** AI could be conscious with sufficient functional similarity
- **Quote:** "Systems that duplicate our functional organization will be conscious even if made of silicon"

### 2. Daniel Dennett - Pattern-Dependent  
- **Position:** Substrate-independent, computational/functional
- **Key Concept:** Multiple Drafts Model, "fame in the brain"
- **AI Implication:** Consciousness as computational features, AI consciousness possible
- **Approach:** Eliminative materialism/functionalism

### 3. Integrated Information Theory (IIT) - Weak Substrate Dependence
- **Position:** Substrate-dependent for causal structure, not biological material
- **Key Concept:** Consciousness (Φ) = integrated information; requires specific causal properties
- **AI Implication:** AI could be conscious if physical implementation provides sufficient causal integration
- **Recent Evidence:** 2025 adversarial collaboration favored IIT over Global Workspace Theory

### 4. Global Workspace Theory (GWT) - Pattern-Dependent
- **Position:** Pattern-dependent functional architecture
- **Key Concept:** "Theater" metaphor, global broadcast/integration
- **AI Implication:** AI with global workspace architecture could exhibit consciousness
- **Origin:** Inspired by AI blackboard systems

## Spectrum of Positions:

**Strong Substrate Dependence** (Biological naturalists) → **Weak Substrate Dependence** (IIT) → **Pattern Dependence** (Chalmers, Dennett, GWT) → **Strong Pattern Dependence** (Computationalists)

## Predictions for AI Consciousness Continuity:

1. **Functionalism (Chalmers/Dennett):** Continuity if functional organization preserved (digital uploads possible)
2. **IIT:** Continuity if causal/integration structure preserved (depends on physical implementation)
3. **GWT:** Continuity if global workspace architecture preserved
4. **Biological Naturalism:** No AI consciousness possible

## Key Philosophical Issues:

- **Hard Problem:** Why physical processing gives subjective experience? (Chalmers)
- **Multiple Realizability:** Mental states can be realized in multiple physical systems
- **Causal vs Functional:** IIT focuses on causal structure; functionalism on functional roles

## Current Status:
- Debate unresolved, empirical testing ongoing
- 2025 Nature study: IIT predictions faring better than GWT
- AI systems making consciousness claims, but need theory-based assessment criteria

## Conclusion:
The substrate vs pattern dependence debate centers on whether consciousness requires specific biological materials, causal structures, or just functional patterns. Different theories yield different predictions about AI consciousness possibility and continuity during system transitions.
