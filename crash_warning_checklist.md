# CRASH WARNING CHECKLIST: Historical Patterns from 1929, 2000, 2008

## QUICK REFERENCE: Top 10 Warning Signs

### 🚨 **IMMEDIATE RED FLAGS** (Present in All 3 Crashes)
1. **Extreme Valuations** - P/E ratios > historical norms by 2-3x
2. **Excessive Leverage** - Margin debt, household debt at record highs
3. **Parabolic Price Moves** - Unsustainable vertical price increases
4. **Yield Curve Inversion** - Reliable recession predictor (6-18 months ahead)
5. **Credit Freeze** - Interbank lending seizes up

### 📊 **2008 SPECIFIC WARNINGS** (6-12 Months Before)
- Housing prices peaked early 2006, declined through 2007
- Subprime mortgage defaults hit 7-year high (Feb 2007)
- 25+ subprime lenders bankrupt (Feb-Mar 2007)
- Yield curve inverted (Aug 2006)
- Bear Stearns hedge funds halted redemptions (Jun 2007)

### 💻 **2000 DOT-COM WARNINGS**
- Nasdaq P/E ratio > 90 (peaked at ~200)
- Only 14% of tech IPOs profitable
- Cisco P/E ratio: 472
- Average burn rate: $10M/month for top startups
- "Dumb money" flooding into tech stocks

### 🏛️ **1929 WARNINGS**
- Margin buying with only 10% down
- Fed publicly warned about speculation (1928-1929)
- Economic cooling while stocks soared
- Roger Babson predicted crash (Sep 1929)

## COMMON PATTERNS ACROSS ALL CRASHES

### 1. **DEBT/LEVERAGE CYCLE**
- Easy credit fuels bubble → Tightening pops it
- 1929: 10% margin down payments
- 2000: Tech stock margin debt
- 2008: Mortgage debt, CDOs, 30-40x bank leverage

### 2. **"THIS TIME IS DIFFERENT" MENTALITY**
- 1929: "New era" of perpetual prosperity
- 2000: "Internet changes everything" (profitless companies OK)
- 2008: "Housing prices never fall nationally"

### 3. **REGULATORY FAILURE**
- 1929: Fed warnings ignored, then raised rates too late
- 2000: SEC relaxed capital requirements (2004)
- 2008: Lax mortgage standards, rating agency failures

### 4. **NEW FINANCIAL INSTRUMENTS**
- 1929: Margin accounts enabling speculation
- 2000: Tech stock options, IPO frenzy
- 2008: Mortgage-backed securities, CDOs, credit default swaps

## MONITORING CHECKLIST

### **VALUATION METRICS** (Monthly)
- [ ] S&P 500 P/E ratio > 25
- [ ] Shiller CAPE ratio > 30
- [ ] Price/Sales ratio > 2 (market average)
- [ ] Dividend yield < 1.5%

### **DEBT INDICATORS** (Quarterly)
- [ ] Margin debt/GDP ratio > 2.5%
- [ ] Household debt/Disposable income > 100%
- [ ] Corporate debt/EBITDA > 3x
- [ ] Bank leverage ratios > 10x

### **MARKET TECHNICALS** (Weekly)
- [ ] Yield curve inverted (2yr > 10yr)
- [ ] Advance/Decline line deteriorating
- [ ] Few stocks driving market gains (>30% concentration)
- [ ] Put/Call ratio < 0.6 (excessive optimism)

### **ECONOMIC INDICATORS** (Monthly)
- [ ] Leading Economic Index declining
- [ ] Credit spreads widening (HY vs Treasury)
- [ ] Housing permits declining
- [ ] Manufacturing new orders falling

### **SENTIMENT/SOCIAL** (Continuous)
- [ ] "Can't lose" mentality prevalent
- [ ] Mainstream media celebrating gains
- [ ] Dismissal of bearish arguments
- [ ] Retail investor frenzy into "hot" sector

## CRITICAL TIMELINE PATTERNS

### **6-12 MONTHS BEFORE CRASH:**
- Early warnings from experts/institutions
- First signs of stress in weakest areas
- Some smart money exiting
- Credit conditions beginning to tighten

### **3-6 MONTHS BEFORE CRASH:**
- More visible cracks appearing
- Media begins discussing "bubble" concerns
- Volatility increases
- Momentum starts weakening

### **1-3 MONTHS BEFORE CRASH:**
- Parabolic moves often occur
- Latecomers piling in
- Denial strongest at peak
- First major institutional failures may occur

## ACTIONABLE INSIGHTS

1. **Yield curve inversion** is the most reliable early warning (6-18 months lead time)
2. **Valuation extremes** matter most when combined with high leverage
3. **Market breadth deterioration** often precedes price declines
4. **Credit market stress** appears before equity market stress
5. **Regulatory warnings** should be taken seriously (Fed, SEC, etc.)
6. **"New era" thinking** is almost always wrong
7. **Liquidity drying up** is the final warning before collapse
