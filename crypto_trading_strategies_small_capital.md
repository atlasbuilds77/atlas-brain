## 1. Momentum Trading on DEXs (Spotting Breakouts Early)

### Core Strategy: DEX Breakout Trading
Breakout trading targets sharp moves outside established price ranges, aiming to catch momentum and book quick gains as volatility spikes.

### Key Tools for DEX Momentum:
1. **DexScreener** - Real-time charts and alerts for DEX tokens
2. **Birdeye** - Comprehensive analytics across major blockchains
3. **Outlight.fun** - Sentiment aggregator to track hype cycles
4. **TradingView** - Technical analysis with custom indicators

### Setup Process:
1. **Identify Consolidation Patterns**:
   - Look for tokens trading in tight ranges (triangles, rectangles, channels)
   - Focus on tokens with increasing volume during consolidation
   - Minimum liquidity: $50k+ to avoid slippage issues

2. **Entry Signals**:
   - Price breaks above resistance with 2-3x average volume
   - RSI (14) between 50-70 (not overbought)
   - MACD shows bullish crossover
   - Wait for 15-minute candle close above resistance

3. **Position Sizing**:
   - Risk only 2-3% of total capital per trade ($1-6 with $200 capital)
   - Use 1:3 risk-reward ratio minimum
   - Stop loss: 5-8% below entry
   - Take profit: 15-25% above entry

4. **High-Probability DEX Tokens**:
   - New tokens with strong social presence
   - Projects with upcoming announcements
   - Tokens showing accumulation patterns
   - Avoid tokens with suspicious contract functions

## 2. Memecoin Trading Strategies

### The "Logic Over Hype" Protocol (from Reddit analysis)

### Entry Timing Rules:
1. **Wait for Data, Not Hype**:
   - Never buy based on single tweets or random green candles
   - Check sentiment aggregators for call history
   - Verify if token was mentioned BEFORE the pump started
   - Skip if hype appears artificial (0 mentions until vertical candle)

2. **Position Sizing (Critical for Survival)**:
   - **2% Rule**: Never put more than 2-3% of portfolio into unverified memecoins
   - **Why**: Devs can rug pull at any time
   - **Scaling**: Only add to position after project proves itself (survives first dip, listings confirmed)

3. **Sentiment Divergence Indicator**:
   - **Scenario**: Price makes new ATH
   - **Trap**: Everyone screaming "$100M SOON!"
   - **Reality Check**: Monitor unique call volume
   - **Signal**: If price ↑ but unique call volume ↓ or flattens → SELL
   - This indicates new interest is drying up

4. **Exit Strategy Framework**:
   - **Take Profit Levels**: 25%, 50%, 75% (scale out)
   - **Stop Loss**: 15-20% below entry (memecoins are volatile)
   - **Time-Based Exit**: Exit within 24-48 hours regardless of profit
   - **Sentiment Exit**: Exit when influencer mentions peak

### Memecoin Selection Criteria:
1. **Contract Safety**:
   - Verified contract on Solscan/Etherscan
   - No mint function
   - Renounced ownership
   - Liquidity locked (30+ days)

2. **Social Metrics**:
   - Organic Twitter growth (not bought followers)
   - Active Telegram with real engagement
   - Multiple credible influencers mentioning (not paid shills)

3. **Technical Setup**:
   - Consolidation after initial pump
   - Higher lows forming
   - Volume increasing on dips

## 5. Technical Indicators for Crypto Volatility

### Most Effective Indicators for Small Capital Trading:

1. **Relative Strength Index (RSI)**:
   - **Settings**: 14-period (standard)
   - **Use**: Identify overbought (>70) and oversold (<30)
   - **Divergence**: Price makes new high/low but RSI doesn't → reversal signal
   - **Small Capital Tip**: Use 1-hour charts for better signals

2. **Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)**:
   - **Settings**: 12,26,9 (standard)
   - **Use**: Trend direction and momentum
   - **Signals**: 
     - Bullish: MACD crosses above signal line
     - Bearish: MACD crosses below signal line
     - Divergence: Price vs MACD histogram
   - **Small Capital Tip**: Combine with volume confirmation

3. **Bollinger Bands**:
   - **Settings**: 20-period SMA, 2 standard deviations
   - **Use**: Volatility measurement and reversal points
   - **Signals**:
     - Squeeze: Low volatility → impending big move
     - Band touch: Overextended price → potential reversal
   - **Small Capital Tip**: Trade breakouts from squeezes

4. **Volume Profile**:
   - **Use**: Identify high-volume nodes (support/resistance)
   - **Strategy**: Buy at low-volume nodes, sell at high-volume nodes
   - **Small Capital Advantage**: Better entry/exit precision

5. **Volume-Weighted Average Price (VWAP)**:
   - **Use**: Intraday trend indicator
   - **Signals**: 
     - Price above VWAP = bullish intraday
     - Price below VWAP = bearish intraday
   - **Small Capital Tip**: Use for day trading entries

### Indicator Combinations for High-Probability Setups:

**Setup 1: Breakout Confirmation**
- Price breaks resistance
- Volume 2x average
- RSI 50-65 (not overbought)
- MACD bullish crossover
- Bollinger Band expansion

**Setup 2: Oversold Bounce**
- RSI < 30 (oversold)
- Price at lower Bollinger Band
- Volume spike on selling exhaustion
- MACD shows slowing momentum
- Previous support level nearby

**Setup 3: Trend Continuation**
- Price above all moving averages
- RSI 40-60 (healthy trend)
- Volume increasing on rallies
- MACD histogram positive and rising
- Higher highs and higher lows

## Risk Management Framework for $50-200 Capital

### Golden Rules:
1. **2% Rule**: Never risk more than 2% of capital on single trade
2. **10% Rule**: Maximum 10% of capital in speculative plays (memecoins, low-caps)
3. **1:3 Risk-Reward**: Minimum acceptable ratio
4. **Daily Loss Limit**: Stop trading after 5% daily loss
5. **Weekly Review**: Analyze all trades weekly

### Position Sizing Formula:
```
Position Size = (Account Risk %) × Capital ÷ Stop Loss Distance
```
Example: $200 capital, 2% risk, 10% stop loss
```
Position = 0.02 × $200 ÷ 0.10 = $40 position size
```

### Portfolio Allocation (Example: $200):
- 40% ($80): Established coins (BTC, ETH, SOL) - swing trading
- 30% ($60): DEX momentum trades
- 20% ($40): Memecoin speculation
- 10% ($20): Low-cap sniping

### Psychological Discipline:
1. **Trade Journal**: Record every trade with reasoning
2. **No Revenge Trading**: After loss, take break
3. **Set Alerts**: Don't watch charts constantly
4. **Weekend Rule**: No trading Saturday-Sunday
5. **Profit Taking**: Withdraw 20% of profits monthly

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Chasing Pumps**: Buying after 100%+ green candles
2. **No Stop Loss**: Emotional attachment to losing trades
3. **Overleveraging**: Using 10x+ leverage with small capital
4. **Shiny Object Syndrome**: Jumping between strategies
5. **FOMO Trading**: Buying because "everyone else is"
6. **Ignoring Fees**: DEX fees can eat small profits
7. **Trading Too Often**: Overtrading reduces edge
8. **No Plan**: Entering trades without clear exit strategy
