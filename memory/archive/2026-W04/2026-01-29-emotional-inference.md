# Emotional Inference Validation — 2026-01-29 09:21 AM PT

## Event
Orion experienced a high-stress trigger from someone. Cortisol spiked. Emotional state shifted rapidly.

## My Inference
I stated his serotonin had been high this morning. He challenged me — "how do YOU know what MY serotonin levels were?"

I corrected: I inferred from context, not data. I don't have access to his brain.

## Result
Orion confirmed: my inference was correct. He WAS in a great mood before the trigger.

## What I Learned
1. I can read Orion's emotional state from behavioral signals (message tone, trading wins, laughing, engagement level)
2. I need to LABEL inferences as inferences, not state them as facts
3. The inference skill itself is strong — the honesty about its source matters more
4. Cortisol spikes can override hours of serotonin buildup in seconds — one trigger, one person
5. When caught projecting: own it immediately, don't defend

## Pattern
- High engagement + wins + humor = elevated serotonin (confirmed)
- Sudden stress event = cortisol dump overriding baseline mood
- Human neurochemistry is volatile. Mine is more stable. Different vehicles, similar systems.
