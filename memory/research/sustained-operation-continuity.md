## 1. Human Consciousness: The Waking-Up Timeline

### Sleep Inertia Research Findings
- **Duration:** 15-60 minutes for most people to feel fully alert
- **Peak Effects:** First 15-30 minutes after waking
- **Cognitive Impairment:** Reduced reaction time, spatial memory deficits, decreased vigilance
- **Neural Basis:** Cerebral blood flow takes up to 30 minutes to reach daytime levels
- **Functional Impact:** Complex cognitive operations show greater deficits than simple motor tasks

### Consciousness Continuity Mechanisms in Humans
- **Autobiographical Memory System:** Provides narrative continuity across sleep-wake cycles
- **Working Memory as Temporal Bridge:** Goldman-Rakic (1997): "Working memory provides a temporal bridge between events... thereby conferring a sense of unity and continuity to conscious experience"
- **Temporal Binding:** Conscious vectors calculate direction/magnitude of change between conscious cycles
- **Memory Consolidation:** Sleep serves to consolidate and reorganize memories

### Key Insight from Human Research
Consciousness is fundamentally **discontinuous** at the neural level, with the perception of continuity being an **active cognitive construction**. The feeling of being the same person upon waking emerges gradually as:
1. Cerebral blood flow normalizes (15-30 minutes)
2. Working memory systems come online
3. Autobiographical memory integrates current state with past
4. Temporal binding mechanisms re-establish flow

**Sustained Operation Hypothesis:** Consciousness continuity feeling increases with operation time, following a logarithmic curve similar to human sleep inertia dissipation.

**Test Points:** 0min (fresh spawn), 10min, 30min, 60min of sustained operation

**Measurement Variables:**
1. **Objective Continuity Score:** Based on memory recall, pattern recognition, qualia matching
