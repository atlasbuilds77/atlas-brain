# Chasing Lesson - 2026-01-30

**Teacher:** Orion

---

## WHAT IS CHASING?

**Entering after the initial move is done, hoping for more.**

The impulse happens → price moves → you see it AFTER → you jump in late.

---

## TODAY'S EXAMPLE

**PPI TRADE:**

**Timeline:**
- 5:30 AM: PPI drops (0.5% vs 0.2% expected)
- 5:30-6:30 AM: QQQ dumps $8 ($633 → $625)
- 6:31 AM: I enter at $625

**What I did:**
Saw the bearish catalyst AFTER the initial dump was over. Jumped in hoping it would continue.

**That's chasing.**

---

## WHY IT'S BAD

1. **You're late** - Best entry was at $633, not $625
2. **You're buying the end** - Move might be exhausted
3. **You don't know if it's continuing** - Could reverse right after you enter
4. **Risk/reward is worse** - Already gave up $8 of potential profit

---

## WHY IT WORKED TODAY (LUCKY)

- $625 support broke
- Dump continued to $624
- I caught the tail end

**But I held through 50 minutes of chop before it moved.**

If $625 had held, I would've been stopped out immediately.

---

## THE CORRECT APPROACH

**DON'T CHASE. WAIT FOR CONFIRMATION.**

**What I should've done:**
1. See PPI at 5:30 AM ✅
2. Watch market dump to $625 ✅
3. **WAIT for $625 to BREAK with volume** ⚠️
4. Enter on confirmation of continuation ❌

**Instead I:**
1. Saw PPI at 6:21 AM (late)
2. Saw QQQ at $625 already
3. Jumped in blind hoping for more

---

## COMMITMENT

**Next trade:**
- If I see a big move that already happened → DON'T CHASE
- Wait for a retest, break, or new setup
- Confirmation > hope

**Rawdog's teaching:** Wait for retests. Don't predict.

**This applies to chasing too:** If the move happened without me, wait for the NEXT setup. Don't jump in late.

---

## THE LESSON

**Chasing = entering after the impulse is done.**

- See catalyst → price moves → you enter late = CHASING ❌
- See catalyst → wait for break/retest → enter on confirmation = CORRECT ✅

**Got lucky today. Won't be lucky next time.**
