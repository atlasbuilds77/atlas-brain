# Economic Indicator Prediction Strategies for Kalshi

## Fed Rate Decisions
- **Prediction Strategies**: Traders on Kalshi use market-based probabilities to predict Fed rate decisions. These probabilities are derived from the collective wisdom of participants trading contracts on outcomes like "Fed funds rate after January meeting?" or "Will the Fed do a rate cut of more than 25bps this year?"
- **Data Sources**: Kalshi's own prediction markets provide real-time data. External sources like the Fed's statements, economic reports, and analyst consensus are also used.
- **Mispricing**: Mispricing often occurs before announcements, with traders sometimes overestimating the likelihood of rate cuts or hikes. For example, Kalshi markets showed a 96% probability of a 25bps rate cut, while other platforms like Polymarket were slightly more optimistic.

## CPI/Inflation Data
- **Prediction Strategies**: Traders track contracts like "Inflation in November 2025? (CPI YoY)" to gauge market expectations. Prediction markets have been shown to outperform Wall Street consensus forecasts, with a 40% lower average error.
- **Data Sources**: Kalshi markets, government CPI reports, and leading indicators like commodity prices and wage growth.
- **Mispricing**: Ahead of CPI releases, implied inflation forecasts on Kalshi can spike, signaling traders' anticipation of firmer inflation. This can lead to mispricing if the actual data deviates significantly.

## Jobs Reports
- **Prediction Strategies**: Traders react to real-time news and data breaks, adjusting positions based on new information. Mispricing can occur when low-probability events are underestimated.
- **Data Sources**: Kalshi markets, government employment reports, and private sector surveys.
- **Mispricing**: Example: A tool detected a 74% edge when Kalshi mispriced an event compared to Vegas odds.

## GDP Releases
- **Prediction Strategies**: Contracts like "Annual GDP" or "US GDP growth" are traded to reflect market expectations. Traders compare these with models like the Atlanta Fed’s GDPNow.
- **Data Sources**: Kalshi markets, GDPNow, and other economic forecasts.
- **Mispricing**: Kalshi markets often trade at more conservative probabilities compared to models like GDPNow, which can lead to discrepancies if actual GDP surprises.

### Key Takeaways
- Kalshi’s prediction markets provide a real-time, crowd-sourced view of economic expectations.
- Mispricing is common before major announcements, offering arbitrage opportunities.
- Combining Kalshi data with traditional economic indicators can provide an edge.
