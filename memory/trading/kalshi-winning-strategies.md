## THE BIASES THAT MAKE PEOPLE LOSE (72M Trades Analyzed)

### 1. Longshot Bias
- People consistently OVERPAY for low-probability outcomes
- 5-cent contracts only hit 4.18% of the time (not 5%)
- **STRATEGY: Bet AGAINST longshots (buy NO on hyped low-prob events)**

### 2. Maker vs Taker Advantage
- MAKERS (limit orders) earn ~2.5% excess returns per trade
- TAKERS (market orders) LOSE that same amount
- NBA Finals analysis: Takers lost $7M aggregate betting on longshots
- **STRATEGY: ALWAYS use limit orders. Be the maker.**

### 3. Yogi Berra Bias ("It Ain't Over Till It's Over")
- Traders refuse to update beliefs when outcome is mathematically certain
- They double down on dead positions (team down 20 with 1 min left)
- **STRATEGY: Cut losses quickly. Don't hold hopeless positions.**

### 4. Optimism Tax (Default Bias)
- People prefer buying YES (positive outcome, default option)
- YES contracts are often disadvantaged
- **STRATEGY: Look for edge in NO positions**

### 5. Emotional vs Boring Categories
- EMOTIONAL (Sports, Crypto, Entertainment) = LESS EFFICIENT = MORE EDGE
- BORING (Finance, Fed rates) = MORE EFFICIENT = HARDER TO BEAT
- **STRATEGY: Target emotional categories for mispricing**
