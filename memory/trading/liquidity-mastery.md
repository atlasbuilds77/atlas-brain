# ICT Liquidity - Complete Mastery Guide

## What is Liquidity in Trading?

**Liquidity** = **Where stop losses are sitting**

In ICT methodology, liquidity refers to clusters of stop-loss orders that institutional traders TARGET to fill their large positions. Think of it as "fuel" that big money needs to enter or exit the market.

**Core principle:** Price doesn't move randomly. It moves to where the money is—and the money is in retail traders' stop losses.

---

## Why Liquidity Matters

### The Institutional Problem

**Challenge:** Banks and hedge funds need to move billions of dollars. If they simply "market buy," they'd move price against themselves dramatically.

**Solution:** They manipulate price to trigger retail stop losses, which provides them the liquidity (counterparty orders) they need.

### The Manipulation Cycle

1. **Retail traders** place obvious stops below support or above resistance
2. **Institutions** know exactly where these stops are
3. **Price is driven** to these levels to trigger the stops ("Stop Hunt")
4. **Institutions** use this liquidity to fill their positions
5. **Real move** begins in the opposite direction
6. **Retail traders** are caught on the wrong side

**This happens thousands of times per day across all markets.**

## Types of Liquidity

### 1. Buy-Side Liquidity (BSL)

**Location:** Above swing highs, resistance levels, round numbers

**Who's there:** 
- Retail short sellers with stop losses
- Breakout traders with buy stops

**What it looks like:**
- Previous highs
- Equal highs (double top, triple top)
- Psychological levels (1.1000, 1.2000, etc.)
- Ascending trendlines

**Color-coding:** Draw in **green** or **blue** on your chart

### 2. Sell-Side Liquidity (SSL)

**Location:** Below swing lows, support levels, round numbers

**Who's there:**
- Retail long traders with stop losses
- Breakdown traders with sell stops

**What it looks like:**
- Previous lows
- Equal lows (double bottom, triple bottom)
- Psychological levels
- Descending trendlines

**Color-coding:** Draw in **red** or **orange** on your chart

## Liquidity Pools

**Definition:** Areas where CLUSTERS of stop losses are concentrated

### How to Identify Liquidity Pools

#### 1. Equal Highs/Lows (Strongest Signal) ⭐⭐⭐

**Equal Highs:**
- Two or more swing highs at approximately the same level
- "Double top" or "triple top"
- Massive buy-side liquidity sitting above

**Equal Lows:**
- Two or more swing lows at approximately the same level
- "Double bottom" or "triple bottom"
- Massive sell-side liquidity sitting below

**Why they're powerful:** Retail traders love these patterns. They place stops just above/below, creating huge liquidity pools.

#### 2. Previous Day/Week Highs & Lows

- Daily high/low
- Weekly high/low
- Monthly high/low

**Key:** Asian session often sets the range; London/NY sweeps the extremes

#### 3. Obvious Support/Resistance

- Levels where price bounced multiple times
- Clear horizontal levels
- Retail traders see these and place stops just beyond them

#### 4. Round Numbers (Psychological Levels)

- 1.1000, 1.2000 (forex)
- 4000, 4100, 4200 (indices)
- Retail traders love round numbers for stop placement

#### 5. Trendlines

- Ascending trendlines = SSL builds below them
- Descending trendlines = BSL builds above them
- When price breaks trendline → liquidity taken

## Liquidity Sweeps

**Definition:** When price briefly moves through a level to trigger stops, then REVERSES

### The Anatomy of a Liquidity Sweep

**Phase 1: Setup**
- Price approaches liquidity pool (equal lows in uptrend)
- Retail traders place stops below

**Phase 2: The Sweep (Stop Hunt)**
- Price rapidly drops below the lows
- Stop losses are triggered
- Retail longs are stopped out
- Retail shorts enter (thinking breakdown)

**Phase 3: The Reversal**
- Price immediately reverses
- Sharp move in opposite direction
- Institutions now have filled positions
- Real move begins

**Phase 4: The Trap**
- Retail traders who entered short are now underwater
- Price continues higher
- Their stops (buy orders) fuel the upward move further

### Visual Example of Bullish Liquidity Sweep

```
     4. REAL MOVE UP ↗️
        (Institutions long)
         /
        /  3. REVERSAL 
       /   (Stop triggered, reverses)
      /    
  ___/____ ← Equal Lows (Liquidity Pool)
     2. SWEEP ↓ (Takes stops)
     
1. Setup: Price approaches lows
2. Sweep: Drops below to trigger stops
3. Reversal: Immediately rebounds
4. Real Move: Continues higher
```

### How to Identify a Sweep (Real-Time)

✅ **Valid Sweep Characteristics:**

1. **Wick below/above the level** (not just the close)
2. **Quick in and out** (1-5 candles)
3. **Strong reversal candle** (engulfing, pin bar)
4. **Close back inside the range**
5. **Occurs during Kill Zone** (London/NY open)

❌ **Not a Sweep:**
- Slow grind through the level
- Multiple closes beyond the level
- No reversal
- Happens during low-volume times

## Liquidity Runs

**Definition:** When price moves through liquidity and CONTINUES (not a sweep)

### Sweep vs. Run (Critical Difference)

#### Liquidity SWEEP (Reversal)
- Price takes liquidity
- **IMMEDIATELY reverses**
- Opposite of trend continuation
- Usually means trend change or strong pullback

#### Liquidity RUN (Continuation)
- Price takes liquidity
- **CONTINUES in same direction**
- Confirms trend strength
- Used to reach next liquidity pool

### When to Expect a Run vs. Sweep

**Expect a SWEEP (Reversal) when:**
- Price is at opposite-trend liquidity
- In uptrend → equal lows are swept
- In downtrend → equal highs are swept
- Price is at premium/discount extreme
- Higher timeframe bias is opposite

**Expect a RUN (Continuation) when:**
- Price is at same-trend liquidity
- In uptrend → equal highs are run
- In downtrend → equal lows are run
- Trend is very strong (multiple BOS)
- Higher timeframe strongly aligned

### Example: Uptrend Scenario

**Setup:** Strong bullish market structure

**Liquidity below (SSL):**
- Price sweeps it → **Pullback, then continues up** (Sweep)
- This is a buying opportunity

**Liquidity above (BSL):**
- Price runs through it → **Continues up to next level** (Run)
- Not a reversal signal

## Trading Liquidity Sweeps (Step-by-Step)

### The High-Probability Liquidity Sweep Trade

This is one of the BEST ICT setups. Here's exactly how to execute it:

#### Step 1: Identify Market Structure & Bias

**Ask:**
- What's the higher timeframe trend? (Daily, 4H)
- Bullish or bearish?

**In uptrend:** Look for sell-side liquidity sweeps (below equal lows)
**In downtrend:** Look for buy-side liquidity sweeps (above equal highs)

#### Step 2: Mark Liquidity Pools

**On 15M or 1H chart:**
- Mark equal highs and equal lows
- Mark previous session highs/lows
- Mark obvious support/resistance

**Use horizontal lines or boxes**

#### Step 3: Wait for Kill Zone

**Best times:**
- London Open: 2-5 AM NY
- NY Open: 7-10 AM NY

**DO NOT trade outside these times for liquidity plays**

#### Step 4: Watch for the Sweep

**What you're looking for:**
- Price rapidly moves toward liquidity pool
- Breaks through the level (wicks through)
- Quickly reverses back inside range
- Strong rejection candle forms

#### Step 5: Identify the Order Block

**Critical:** Where did the reversal start?
- That candle = Order Block
- This is where institutions entered

**Drop to 5M or 1M chart to refine the exact OB**

#### Step 6: Wait for Retest (if needed)

**Sometimes:**
- Price sweeps and reverses immediately → Enter now
- Price sweeps, reverses, then pulls back to OB → Wait for retest

**The retest of the OB is often the safer entry**

#### Step 7: Enter the Trade

**Entry conditions:**
- Price has swept liquidity
- Price has returned to Order Block (or rejecting immediately)
- Lower timeframe (1M) shows structure shift

**Entry execution:**
- Market order when OB is touched
- Or limit order at OB level

#### Step 8: Set Stop Loss

**Stop placement:**
- Below the swept low (for longs)
- Above the swept high (for shorts)
- Add 5-10 pip buffer

**Why this works:** If price goes back through, the setup failed

#### Step 9: Set Take Profit

**Target 1:** 50% of range (1:1 or 1:2 RR)
**Target 2:** Opposing liquidity pool
**Target 3:** Previous swing high/low

**Recommended:** Scale out at each target

## Real Trade Examples

### Example 1: Bullish Liquidity Sweep (LONG)

**Scenario:** EUR/USD in uptrend

**Setup:**
1. Daily chart shows bullish structure
2. 1H chart: Price in discount zone (below 50% Fib)
3. 15M chart: Equal lows at 1.0950 (sell-side liquidity)
4. Wait for NY open (7 AM)

**Execution:**
1. 7:15 AM: Price drops to 1.0945 (sweeps the lows)
2. Immediately reverses with strong bullish candle
3. Bullish Order Block identified at 1.0945-1.0950
4. Enter LONG at 1.0950
5. Stop loss: 1.0940 (below sweep)
6. Target 1: 1.0980 (previous resistance)
7. Target 2: 1.1000 (buy-side liquidity / round number)

**Result:** Price rallies to 1.1005. Both targets hit. +50 pips total.

### Example 2: Bearish Liquidity Sweep (SHORT)

**Scenario:** GBP/USD in downtrend

**Setup:**
1. Daily chart shows bearish structure
2. 1H chart: Price in premium zone (above 50% Fib)
3. 15M chart: Equal highs at 1.2700 (buy-side liquidity)
4. Wait for London open (2 AM)

**Execution:**
1. 2:30 AM: Price spikes to 1.2705 (sweeps the highs)
2. Immediately reverses with strong bearish candle
3. Bearish Order Block identified at 1.2700-1.2705
4. Enter SHORT at 1.2700
5. Stop loss: 1.2710 (above sweep)
6. Target 1: 1.2650 (previous support)
7. Target 2: 1.2600 (sell-side liquidity / round number)

**Result:** Price drops to 1.2595. Both targets hit. +105 pips total.

## Advanced Liquidity Concepts

### 1. Internal Liquidity vs. External Liquidity

**Internal Liquidity:**
- Within the current range
- Example: Swing high/low inside consolidation
- Lower priority target

**External Liquidity:**
- Outside the current range
- Example: Previous day high/low, all-time highs
- Higher priority target

**Trading tip:** External liquidity sweeps are more powerful

### 2. Engineered Liquidity

**What it is:** When institutions create a level specifically to build liquidity

**How it works:**
1. Price consolidates at a level
2. Multiple touches create "support" or "resistance"
3. Retail traders place stops just beyond
4. Level breaks → massive liquidity grab
5. This was the plan all along

**How to spot it:** 3+ touches at same level = engineered liquidity pool

### 3. Divergent Liquidity

**Concept:** Two correlated pairs with liquidity at different levels

**Example:**
- EUR/USD has SSL at 1.0900
- GBP/USD has SSL at 1.2600
- Both pairs in downtrend

**What happens:**
- One pair sweeps first (e.g., EUR/USD hits 1.0900)
- Watch the other pair (GBP/USD)
- If GBP/USD doesn't reach its liquidity → SMT divergence
- Trade the weaker pair (GBP/USD long after sweep)

### 4. Liquidity Voids

**What it is:** Areas with NO liquidity

**Characteristics:**
- Fast, impulsive moves
- Very few candles
- Large candles with small wicks
- No consolidation

**Trading implication:**
- Price moves through voids quickly
- Don't place targets in voids
- Don't expect support/resistance in voids

## Combining Liquidity with Other Concepts

### Liquidity + Order Blocks (Best Combo) ⭐⭐⭐

**The Perfect Setup:**
1. Identify liquidity pool
2. Wait for sweep
3. Price reverses from Order Block
4. Enter at OB
5. Target: Opposite liquidity

**This is THE highest probability ICT trade**

### Liquidity + Fair Value Gaps

**Setup:**
1. Price sweeps liquidity
2. FVG is left behind during the sweep
3. Price returns to fill the FVG
4. Enter when FVG is filled

**Works best when FVG overlaps with Order Block**

### Liquidity + SMT Divergence

**Setup:**
1. Two correlated pairs approach their liquidity
2. Both sweep
3. One makes new high/low, other doesn't (SMT)
4. Trade the divergent pair

**Confirmation:** Use this with OB for higher conviction

### Liquidity + Kill Zones

**Critical timing:**
- Asian session: Builds liquidity, don't trade
- London open: Sweeps Asian liquidity (tradeable)
- NY open: Sweeps London liquidity (BEST)
- Lunch: Don't trade
- London close: Sometimes sweeps NY AM liquidity

**Rule:** Only trade liquidity sweeps during London/NY opens

## Common Liquidity Mistakes

❌ **Mistake 1: Trading every liquidity sweep**
- Not all sweeps lead to reversals
**Fix:** Combine with OB, higher timeframe bias, and premium/discount

❌ **Mistake 2: Entering before the sweep**
- Trying to predict the sweep
**Fix:** Wait for price to ACTUALLY sweep, then enter

❌ **Mistake 3: Trading against higher timeframe**
- Selling a sweep in a strong uptrend
**Fix:** Only trade sweeps aligned with higher timeframe bias

❌ **Mistake 4: Ignoring Kill Zones**
- Trading Asian session sweeps
**Fix:** Only trade during London/NY sessions

❌ **Mistake 5: Not using Order Blocks**
- Entering randomly after a sweep
**Fix:** Always identify the OB where reversal occurred

❌ **Mistake 6: Poor stop placement**
- Stop too tight (before the sweep level)
**Fix:** Stop should be beyond the swept level + buffer

## Liquidity Trading Cheat Sheet

### Before Every Trade, Ask:

✅ **Where is liquidity sitting?**
- Equal highs/lows?
- Previous day/week high/low?
- Round numbers?

✅ **Has it been swept yet?**
- If no → Wait for the sweep
- If yes → Look for the OB

✅ **What's the higher timeframe bias?**
- Bullish → Look for SSL sweeps (buy opportunity)
- Bearish → Look for BSL sweeps (sell opportunity)

✅ **Are we in a Kill Zone?**
- London Open (2-5 AM)?
- NY Open (7-10 AM)?
- If no → Don't trade

✅ **Is there an Order Block?**
- Where did reversal start?
- Is price back at the OB?

✅ **Premium or Discount?**
- In uptrend: Only buy discount sweeps
- In downtrend: Only sell premium sweeps

### If ALL answers align → TAKE THE TRADE
