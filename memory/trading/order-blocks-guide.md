# ICT Order Blocks - Complete Trading Guide

## What is an Order Block?

An **Order Block** is a zone on the chart where large institutional orders were placed, causing a strong price move. Think of it as the "footprint" left behind by banks and hedge funds when they enter or exit positions.

**Key concept:** When price returns to an Order Block, institutions often add to their positions or defend the level, creating high-probability trade setups.

---

## The Anatomy of an Order Block

### Bullish Order Block

**Definition:** The last **bearish candle** before a strong bullish impulse move

**Structure:**
1. First candle: Bearish (down)
2. Second candle: Bullish (up) that **completely engulfs** the first
   - Opens below first candle's low
   - Closes above first candle's high
   - Body to body + wick to wick engulfment

**What it means:** Institutions placed massive buy orders in this zone, causing the explosive move up

### Bearish Order Block

**Definition:** The last **bullish candle** before a strong bearish impulse move

**Structure:**
1. First candle: Bullish (up)
2. Second candle: Bearish (down) that **completely engulfs** the first
   - Opens above first candle's high
   - Closes below first candle's low
   - Body to body + wick to wick engulfment

**What it means:** Institutions placed massive sell orders in this zone, causing the explosive move down

## How to Identify VALID Order Blocks

Not every candle before a big move is a valid Order Block. Here's the checklist:

### ✅ Bullish Order Block Requirements

1. **Complete Engulfment**
   - Second candle's low goes below first candle's low
   - Second candle closes above first candle's high
   - Full body and wick engulfment

2. **Imbalance Present**
   - Check lower timeframe (drop down to 1M or 5M)
   - Look for Fair Value Gap (FVG) within the Order Block zone
   - No wicks filling the gap = stronger OB

3. **Structure Shift**
   - Lower timeframe shows change from bearish to bullish
   - Break of structure (BOS) on 1M/5M chart

4. **Strong Impulse Move**
   - The move away from the Order Block should be strong and fast
   - Sharp price rejection = institutional involvement

### ✅ Bearish Order Block Requirements

1. **Complete Engulfment**
   - Second candle's high goes above first candle's high
   - Second candle closes below first candle's low
   - Full body and wick engulfment

2. **Imbalance Present**
   - Check lower timeframe
   - Look for FVG within the OB zone

3. **Structure Shift**
   - Lower timeframe shows change from bullish to bearish

4. **Strong Impulse Move**
   - Sharp, fast move away from the OB

## Types of Order Blocks

### 1. Reversal Order Blocks

**Used when:** Trend is changing direction

**Example - Bullish Reversal:**
- Market in downtrend
- Price reaches demand zone/support
- Bullish Order Block forms
- Market structure shifts to bullish
- Price reverses upward

**How to trade:**
- Wait for price to return to the bullish OB
- Enter long when price tests the zone
- Stop loss: 10-20 pips below OB low
- Target: Previous high or next liquidity pool

### 2. Continuation Order Blocks

**Used when:** Trend is ongoing; appears after pullback

**Example - Bullish Continuation:**
- Market in strong uptrend
- Price pulls back (bearish retracement)
- Bullish Order Block forms during pullback
- Price respects the OB and continues up

**How to trade:**
- Trend is your friend
- Add to existing positions at OB
- Tighter stops (already in profit)
- Target: Next major high

## Order Block Trading Strategies

### Strategy 1: Reversal Trading

**Setup:**
1. Identify trend exhaustion
2. Wait for structure shift (CHoCH)
3. Locate Order Block at the shift point
4. Wait for price to retrace to OB

**Entry Rules:**
- Price must test the OB zone
- Look for rejection candle (pin bar, engulfing)
- Enter on lower timeframe confirmation (1M/5M structure shift)

**Risk Management:**
- Stop: 10-20 pips beyond OB extreme
- Target 1: 50% retracement
- Target 2: Previous swing point
- Target 3: Opposing liquidity pool

**Example Long Trade:**
```
1. Bearish trend breaks structure (CHoCH)
2. Bullish Order Block forms at the break
3. Price moves up, then pulls back to OB
4. Enter long at OB test with 1M confirmation
5. Stop: Below OB low
6. Target: Previous resistance
```

### Strategy 2: Continuation Trading (Trend Following)

**Setup:**
1. Strong trend in place (multiple BOS)
2. Price pulls back (natural retracement)
3. Order Block forms during pullback
4. Price respects OB and continues trend

**Entry Rules:**
- Only trade with the trend
- Wait for pullback to 50%-79% Fibonacci (discount in uptrend)
- OB should be within this discount zone
- Enter on lower timeframe bullish engulfing

**Risk Management:**
- Stop: Just beyond OB (tighter since trend is strong)
- Target 1: Previous high
- Target 2: Next round number
- Trail stop as price moves in your favor

**Example Long Trade in Uptrend:**
```
1. Strong uptrend (HH, HL structure)
2. Price pulls back to 62-79% Fib level
3. Bullish OB forms in discount zone
4. Enter long when price tests OB
5. Stop: Below OB
6. Target: New high
```

### Strategy 3: OB + Liquidity Sweep Combo ⭐⭐⭐ (BEST)

**This is the highest probability setup**

**Setup:**
1. Identify liquidity pool (equal lows in uptrend, equal highs in downtrend)
2. Wait for liquidity sweep (price takes out the stops)
3. Price reverses and returns to Order Block
4. Enter when OB is tested

**Why it works:**
- Institutions swept retail stops (liquidity grab)
- Now they have liquidity to enter large positions
- OB is where they entered
- High probability they defend this level

**Example Long Trade:**
```
1. Uptrend with equal lows (sell-side liquidity)
2. During London/NY session, price sweeps the lows
3. Price reverses sharply (institutions entered)
4. Bullish Order Block forms where reversal started
5. Price retraces to test the OB
6. Enter long with 1M confirmation
7. Stop: Below the swept low
8. Target: Buy-side liquidity (previous highs)
```

**This is ICT's bread and butter setup**

## Advanced Order Block Concepts

### Refined Order Blocks

**What it is:** Within a larger Order Block, find the EXACT candle that caused the move

**How to find:**
1. Identify the larger OB zone (15M or 5M chart)
2. Drop to 1M chart
3. Find the last opposite-colored candle before the impulse
4. That single candle is your refined OB
5. Much tighter entry and stop loss

**Benefits:**
- More precise entries
- Tighter stops = better risk/reward
- Higher win rate

### Failed Order Blocks (Breaker Blocks)

**What happens:** Sometimes an OB fails and price breaks through it

**When this occurs:**
- The failed bullish OB becomes a bearish Breaker Block
- The failed bearish OB becomes a bullish Breaker Block
- These are extremely powerful reversal zones

**Trading failed OBs:**
- If bullish OB fails → flip to bearish bias
- Wait for price to retrace back to the OB
- It now acts as resistance
- Enter short

### Order Block Mitigation

**What it means:** Price returning to "mitigate" (fill/balance) the OB

**Full mitigation:** Price enters 50% of the OB zone
**Partial mitigation:** Price enters top/bottom of OB

**Trading tip:** The first test of an OB is the strongest. Each subsequent test weakens the OB.

## Combining Order Blocks with Other Concepts

### OB + Fair Value Gap (FVG)

**Powerful combo:** OB with an FVG inside it

**Why it works:**
- FVG = imbalance that price wants to fill
- OB = institutional orders
- Together = magnetic pull for price

**How to trade:**
- Identify OB with FVG inside
- Wait for price to return
- Enter when price enters the FVG within the OB
- Target: Opposite liquidity

### OB + SMT Divergence

**Setup:**
- Two correlated pairs (EUR/USD and GBP/USD)
- Both test their respective Order Blocks
- One makes new high/low, the other doesn't (SMT divergence)
- Strong reversal signal

**How to trade:**
- Wait for both pairs at their OBs
- Watch for divergence
- Enter the weaker pair (the one failing to make new high/low)

### OB + Kill Zone

**Timing matters:**

**Best times for OB trades:**
1. **London Open (2-5 AM NY)** - OBs tested for first time
2. **NY Open (7-10 AM NY)** - Highest probability
3. **London Close (10-12 PM NY)** - Reversal OBs

**Avoid:**
- Asian session (low volume)
- Lunch time (12-1 PM NY)
- Late NY session (low volume)

## Step-by-Step: Trading an Order Block

### Pre-Trade Checklist

✅ **1. Identify Market Structure**
- Uptrend, downtrend, or ranging?
- What's the daily bias?

✅ **2. Find the Order Block**
- Is it valid? (engulfment, imbalance, structure shift)
- Is it in premium or discount zone?
- In uptrend: Want bullish OB in discount
- In downtrend: Want bearish OB in premium

✅ **3. Check for Liquidity**
- Has liquidity been swept?
- If not, wait for the sweep

✅ **4. Time Check**
- Are we in a Kill Zone?

✅ **5. Wait for Price to Return**
- Don't chase
- Let price come to the OB

✅ **6. Lower Timeframe Confirmation**
- Drop to 1M or 5M
- Wait for rejection candle or structure shift

✅ **7. Enter the Trade**
- Set stop loss 10-20 pips beyond OB
- Set target at opposing liquidity or swing point

### Entry Triggers

**Aggressive entry:**
- Price touches OB zone
- Enter immediately
- Wider stop

**Conservative entry (recommended):**
- Price touches OB
- Wait for 1M/5M rejection candle
- Enter after close of rejection candle
- Tighter stop

## Common Mistakes Trading Order Blocks

❌ **Mistake 1: Trading weak Order Blocks**
- No imbalance
- No structure shift
- Weak impulse move
**Fix:** Be selective. Only trade high-quality OBs.

❌ **Mistake 2: Buying premium Order Blocks in uptrend**
- Poor risk/reward
**Fix:** Wait for discount OBs only (below 50% Fib).

❌ **Mistake 3: Entering before liquidity sweep**
- Price not ready to reverse yet
**Fix:** Patience. Wait for stops to be taken.

❌ **Mistake 4: Ignoring timeframe confluence**
- Trading 5M OB against daily trend
**Fix:** Always check higher timeframe bias first.

❌ **Mistake 5: Not refining the Order Block**
- Too wide of a zone
- Large stop loss
**Fix:** Drop to 1M chart and refine the exact candle.

❌ **Mistake 6: Trading every Order Block**
- Low probability setups
**Fix:** Wait for A+ setups (OB + liquidity sweep + kill zone + premium/discount alignment).
