## Core Concepts

### 1. SMT (Smart Money Technique) — Divergence
- Compare correlating pairs: NQ and ES
- If NQ makes a new high but ES doesn't → that's divergence
- The weaker one reveals smart money rotation
- Used for **directional bias**, not direct entry

### 2. FVG (Fair Value Gap)
- Gap in price where no trading occurred (imbalance)
- Three-candle pattern: middle candle gaps between high of candle 1 and low of candle 3

### 3. IFVG (Inverse Fair Value Gap)
- An FVG that gets **inverted** — a candle closes THROUGH the gap
- Bullish FVG gets closed above = inverted
- Bearish FVG gets closed below = inverted
- The close through the gap is what makes it "inverse"
- **This is the key confirmation layer**

## Entry Rules

### Step-by-Step Entry Sequence:
1. **Identify FVG** on higher timeframe (confluence/bias)
2. **Candle closes through FVG → creates IFVG**
3. **Evaluate entry price vs IFVG:**
   - If close is acceptable R:R → market order on the close
   - If close is too far from IFVG (bad R:R) → **wait for retrace**
4. **If waiting for retrace:**
   - Price retraces back into the IFVG
   - **DO NOT limit order in the gap**
   - Wait for **reaction** — see price retrace in, then get a "nice close out of it"
   - That close = confirmation price is respecting the IFVG
   - **THEN market order on that confirming candle**
5. **If price doesn't retrace → missed trade → move on**
   - No FOMO, no chasing
   - Next setup

### Timeframe Logic:
- Higher timeframes (15m, 1h) = confluence for trade ideas
- When confluences align → scale down to **1m/2m** for precise entries
- "Candle closes" = relative to the IFVG timeframe
  - 1m IFVG → 1m candle close
  - 5m FVG reaction → 5m candle close

### Execution:
- **Market orders** — almost always
- Manually watching candle close and clicking market order
- Very little spread in futures, minimal slippage
- No automation — discretionary trading where context matters

## Stop Loss Framework

### Soft Stop (Primary):
- Close trade if a candle **closes** above/below the FVG/IFVG
- Not a hard price level — based on candle close behavior
- More nuanced than a fixed stop

### Hard Stop (Safety):
- Always set ~10 points beyond the soft stop level
- Protects against fast moves that blow through soft stop level
- If swing high/low is close to soft stop → just use that as hard stop (cleaner)

### SMT Stop:
- If entering based on SMT + early entry → hard stop at the high/low where SMT was created
