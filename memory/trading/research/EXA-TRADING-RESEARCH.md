### 💡 SECOND-ORDER GREEK DEFINITIONS

```
VANNA = ∂Delta/∂Vol = ∂Vega/∂Spot
├── Measures: Delta sensitivity to IV changes
├── Or: Vega sensitivity to spot price changes
└── Key: Asymmetry risk in big moves

CHARM = ∂Delta/∂Time (Delta decay)
├── Measures: How delta changes with time
├── Effect: ATM options lose delta as expiry approaches
└── Key: Important for hedging overnight

VOLGA (Vomma) = ∂Vega/∂Vol  
├── Measures: Vega sensitivity to vol changes
├── Effect: Far OTM options gain value in vol spikes
└── Key: Wings become valuable in crashes

VETA = ∂Vega/∂Time
├── Measures: How vega decays
└── Key: Long-dated options lose vega faster
```

### 🔥 WHY THIS MATTERS

**Even if delta-neutral AND vega-neutral, you're still exposed!**

Key insight from Quant-Next:
> "With a second order Taylor expansion... even if a position is delta- and vega-neutral, it remains sensitive to spot and volatility changes."

**Vanna Risk**: Big moves in BOTH spot AND vol (crashes!)
**Volga Risk**: Changes in volatility of volatility

### 💡 MAX PAIN THEORY

```
MAX PAIN = Strike where option buyers lose the most
         = Strike where option sellers profit the most
         = Where market makers "want" price to expire

MECHANICS:
1. Calculate total value of puts + calls at each strike
2. Find strike with minimum total value
3. That's the "max pain" strike
```

### 💡 PIN RISK

**Definition:** Uncertainty when underlying is very close to strike at expiration

```
RISK FOR WRITERS:
├── Don't know if option will be exercised
├── Hard to hedge precisely
└── Can create unexpected positions overnight

TRADING IMPLICATIONS:
├── Price tends to "stick" near strikes with high OI
├── Creates support/resistance at popular strikes
└── Gamma increases dramatically as pin approaches
```

### 🔥 ACTIONABLE
- Check max pain before expiration Friday trades
- Heavy OI strikes act as magnets
- Avoid selling options that could pin

### 💡 FOUR ADJUSTMENT TYPES

```
1. ROLL THE TESTED SIDE
├── Tested = side price is approaching
├── Roll to further OTM strike
└── Accepts smaller credit for more room

2. ROLL THE UNTESTED SIDE
├── Bring untested side closer to market
├── Collect additional credit
└── Increases risk if reversal

3. DELTA HEDGING
├── Buy/sell underlying to neutralize delta
├── Maintains original position
└── More complex, requires monitoring

4. ROLL OUT IN TIME  
├── Close current, open further expiration
├── Gives more time for mean reversion
└── Best if structure still valid
```

### 🔥 DECISION FRAMEWORK

```
IF tested side at 25-delta:
  → Consider rolling that side
  
IF delta of overall position > ±15:
  → Consider delta hedge OR roll untested side
  
IF still 2+ weeks to expiry + structure valid:
  → Wait (theta works for you)
  
IF < 1 week + tested:
  → Roll out in time OR close
```
