# Kalshi Scalping Strategies Research
*Research conducted on January 25, 2026 using Exa deep search*

## Executive Summary

This research examines scalping strategies, arbitrage opportunities, high-frequency trading approaches, and risk management techniques for prediction markets, with a focus on Kalshi. The findings reveal a rapidly evolving ecosystem where traditional financial market strategies are being adapted to prediction markets, with significant opportunities for algorithmic trading and cross-platform arbitrage.

## 1. Best Scalping Strategies for Prediction Markets

### Key Findings:
- **Market Making Algorithms**: The most successful scalping strategies involve market making algorithms that provide liquidity. One Hacker News user reported building "a market making algorithm for Kalshi, an event futures exchange."
- **Micro-Arbitrage Opportunities**: Traders exploit small price discrepancies within the same platform. For example, when Kalshi simultaneously displays "Yes @ 51.0¢" and "No @ 49.0¢", creating a 2¢ arbitrage opportunity.
- **Limit Order Strategies**: Successful traders emphasize using limit orders rather than market orders to control entry and exit prices.
- **Niche Specialization**: Traders who focus on specific market categories (politics, weather, entertainment) tend to outperform generalists.

### Academic Insights:
The paper "Makers and Takers: The Economics of the Kalshi Prediction Market" (Bürgi, Deng, Whelan, 2025) provides systematic evidence that:
- Kalshi's contract prices are informative and improve in accuracy as markets approach closing
- Market makers (those posting offers) are relatively well-informed traders seeking positive expected returns
- Market takers (those accepting offers) provide the counterparty liquidity

## 5. Risk Management for Prediction Market Scalping

### Key Risk Factors:

**Market Microstructure Risks:**
- **Longshot Bias**: Traders overpay for low-probability outcomes
- **Maker-Taker Dynamics**: Understanding who has information advantage
- **Liquidity Risk**: Thin markets can lead to significant slippage
- **Event Risk**: Unexpected news can cause rapid price movements

**Platform-Specific Risks:**
- **Regulatory Uncertainty**: Kalshi faces ongoing regulatory challenges (e.g., Connecticut ordering cessation of sports betting)
- **Counterparty Risk**: Platform solvency and withdrawal capabilities
- **Technical Risk**: API reliability and platform stability

### Risk Management Strategies:

**Position Sizing:**
- Conservative position sizing relative to account size
- Maximum loss limits per trade and per day
- Correlation-aware portfolio construction

**Execution Risk Management:**
- Use of limit orders to control entry/exit prices
- Avoiding market orders in illiquid conditions
- Staggered entry and exit strategies

**Operational Risk:**
- Redundant systems for critical trading infrastructure
- Regular withdrawal of profits from trading platforms
- Diversification across multiple prediction market platforms

### Institutional Adoption:
- Underdog Sports plans to use Kalshi for its own risk management
- This indicates growing acceptance of prediction markets as legitimate risk management tools
- Institutional participation may increase market efficiency and reduce arbitrage opportunities
