# Stock Momentum Trading Research (2024-2026)

## Executive Summary
Momentum trading remains a popular strategy for active traders seeking to capitalize on short-term price trends. This research covers current momentum trading strategies, technical indicators, sector rotation approaches, pre-market/after-hours plays, and available scanning tools for 2024-2026.

## 1. Momentum Trading Strategies (2024-2026)

### Current Effective Strategies

**1.1 Global Equities Momentum Strategy**
- Originally called "Enhanced" Global Growth Cycle (GGC) strategy
- Focuses on tactical asset allocation across global markets
- Uses momentum signals to rotate between international equity markets

**1.2 MACD Momentum Strategy**
- Leverages Moving Average Convergence Divergence indicator
- Identifies shifts in momentum and trend direction
- Popular for both entry and exit timing
- Combines 12-day and 26-day EMAs with 9-day signal line

**1.3 Moving Average-Based Strategies**
- Uses short-term moving averages (9 or 20 period EMA)
- Price held above ascending moving average indicates buyer control
- Simple yet effective for trend confirmation

**1.4 Breakout Momentum Strategy**
- Focuses on stocks breaking through key resistance levels
- High volume breakouts with sustained momentum
- Often combined with news catalysts

**1.5 Mean Reversion Momentum Strategy**
- Identifies overextended moves for potential reversals
- Uses RSI extremes (below 30/above 70) as signals
- Requires careful timing and risk management

## 2. Technical Indicators for Momentum

### Primary Momentum Indicators

**2.1 Relative Strength Index (RSI)**
- Measures speed and magnitude of recent price changes
- 14-day period common for balancing sensitivity and noise
- Key levels: Below 30 (oversold), Above 70 (overbought)
- Divergences between price and RSI signal potential reversals

**2.2 Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)**
- Subtracts 26-day EMA from 12-day EMA
- Above zero: Upward momentum; Below zero: Downward momentum
- Signal line (9-day EMA of MACD) provides crossover signals
- Divergence indicates momentum strength

**2.3 Average Directional Index (ADX)**
- Measures trend strength (0-100 scale)
- Below 20: Nontrending/sideways market
- Above 20-25: Potential entry point
- Above 25: Strong trend; Above 40: Very strong trend
- Often used with RSI for confirmation

**2.4 Commodity Channel Index (CCI)**
- Identifies cyclical trends
- Measures current price relative to average price
- Useful for spotting overbought/oversold conditions

**2.5 Momentum Indicator (MOM)**
- Simple calculation: Current price - price n periods ago
- Direct measure of price velocity
- Zero line crossovers signal momentum shifts

### Secondary Confirmation Indicators
- Volume analysis (unusual volume spikes)
- Bollinger Bands (volatility-based momentum)
- Stochastic Oscillator
- On Balance Volume (OBV)

## 3. Sector Rotation Strategies

### Momentum-Based Sector Rotation

**3.1 Economic Cycle Rotation**
- Rotates into sectors showing strong price momentum
- Uses technical indicators like moving averages and relative strength
- Identifies leading sectors poised for growth

**3.2 Risk-On/Risk-Off Rotation**
- Equal Weight Discretionary Index vs. Equal Weight Staples Index
- Shifts toward risk-on behavior during momentum phases
- Technology to Value rotation observed in 2025 markets

**3.3 Dynamic Sector Rotation Models**
- Designed to outperform S&P 500 momentum index
- Lower drawdown exposure through systematic rotation
- Uses momentum ranking systems

**3.4 Performance-Based Rotation**
- Analyzes sector performance trends
- Identifies sectors gaining/losing momentum
- Times rotations based on economic recovery phases
- Cyclical sectors (industrials, consumer discretionary) often outperform during recoveries

**3.5 Enhanced Returns Strategy**
- Can enhance portfolio returns by 2-4% annually
- Times market cycles and identifies economic phases
- Rotates between technology, utilities, financials, etc.

## 4. Pre-Market/After-Hours Momentum Plays

### Extended Hours Trading (2024-2026 Landscape)

**4.1 Market Structure Changes**
- Shift from standard day plus short pre/post sessions to significantly extended hours
- Regulatory approvals and infrastructure timelines confirmed in early 2025
- Moving toward 'round the clock' trading (24x5)
- Pre-market now accounts for >55% of extended hours trading (up from <17% previously)

**4.2 Trading Sessions**
- **Pre-market**: 4:00 AM - 9:30 AM ET
- **Regular hours**: 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET  
- **After-hours**: 4:00 PM - 8:00 PM ET

**4.3 Momentum Strategies for Extended Hours**

**News Breakout Strategy**
- Identify stocks reacting strongly to breaking news
- Earnings releases, FDA approvals, economic data
- High volatility with rapid price movements

**Gap Trading Strategy**
- Focus on stocks with significant gaps from previous close
- Pre-market gaps often continue into regular session
- Requires understanding of gap fill probabilities

**Continuation Momentum Strategy**
- Stocks showing strong regular session momentum
- Extended hours continuation of trends
- Particularly effective with earnings announcements

**4.4 Key Considerations**
- Lower liquidity in extended hours
- Wider bid-ask spreads
- Higher volatility
- News-driven price action more pronounced

## 6. Risk Management Considerations

### Key Risks in Momentum Trading
- **Rapid reversals**: Can lead to significant losses
- **Lower liquidity**: Especially in extended hours
- **Wider spreads**: Increased transaction costs
- **News volatility**: Unpredictable price reactions
- **Overtrading**: Common pitfall for momentum traders

### Risk Mitigation Strategies
- **Position sizing**: Smaller positions for higher volatility plays
- **Stop losses**: Essential for managing downside risk
- **Diversification**: Across sectors and timeframes
- **News verification**: Confirm catalysts before trading
- **Volume confirmation**: Ensure adequate liquidity

## 9. Conclusion

Momentum trading continues to evolve with market structure changes, particularly the expansion of extended trading hours toward 24x5 markets. Successful momentum trading in 2024-2026 requires:

1. **Adaptation to extended hours**: Pre-market now dominates extended hours volume

The most effective approach combines multiple data points (price, volume, news) with disciplined execution and robust risk management. As markets continue to evolve toward near-24/5 trading, momentum strategies will likely become even more time-sensitive and technology-dependent.
