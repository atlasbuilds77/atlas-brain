# ICT SMT Divergence - Complete Guide

## What is SMT Divergence?

**SMT** = **Smart Money Technique**

**SMT Divergence** occurs when two correlated assets (that normally move together) exhibit OPPOSING price behavior at the same time. One asset makes a new high/low while the other fails to do so.

**Why it matters:** This divergence signals that "Smart Money" (institutions) are positioning for a reversal. It's a powerful confirmation tool for entries and exits.

---

## The Core Concept

### Normal Market Behavior (No Divergence)

**Correlated assets move together:**

Example - EUR/USD and GBP/USD (Positively Correlated):
- EUR/USD makes higher high → GBP/USD makes higher high ✅
- EUR/USD makes lower low → GBP/USD makes lower low ✅
- **Symmetrical movement** = No divergence

### SMT Divergence (Reversal Signal)

**Correlated assets DIVERGE:**

Example - EUR/USD and GBP/USD:
- EUR/USD makes higher high → GBP/USD FAILS to make higher high ❌
- This is **SMT Divergence** = Potential reversal

**What it means:** 
- The "stronger" asset (making new high) is likely a **liquidity sweep**
- The "weaker" asset (failing to make new high) shows **weakness**
- Institutions are NOT buying the high; they're preparing to sell
- Reversal imminent

## Types of SMT Divergence

### 1. Bullish SMT Divergence (Buy Signal)

**In Positively Correlated Assets:**

**Setup:**
- Both assets in downtrend
- Both making lower lows
- Asset A makes a new lower low
- Asset B FAILS to make new lower low (makes higher low instead)

**Signal:** Asset B is showing strength; reversal to upside likely

**Trade:** Enter LONG on Asset B (the weaker/divergent one)

**Example:**
```
EUR/USD: 1.0900 → 1.0850 → 1.0800 (new low)
GBP/USD: 1.2400 → 1.2350 → 1.2370 (HIGHER low) ← SMT Divergence

→ Enter LONG on GBP/USD (it's showing strength)
```

### 2. Bearish SMT Divergence (Sell Signal)

**In Positively Correlated Assets:**

**Setup:**
- Both assets in uptrend
- Both making higher highs
- Asset A makes a new higher high
- Asset B FAILS to make new higher high (makes lower high instead)

**Signal:** Asset B is showing weakness; reversal to downside likely

**Trade:** Enter SHORT on Asset B (the weaker/divergent one)

**Example:**
```
EUR/USD: 1.1000 → 1.1050 → 1.1100 (new high)
GBP/USD: 1.2800 → 1.2850 → 1.2830 (LOWER high) ← SMT Divergence

→ Enter SHORT on GBP/USD (it's showing weakness)
```

### 3. Inverse Correlation Divergence

**For Negatively Correlated Assets (e.g., DXY vs EUR/USD):**

**Bullish SMT (EUR/USD):**
- DXY makes higher low
- EUR/USD makes lower high (instead of higher high)
- EUR/USD is weak → Trade SHORT on EUR/USD

**Bearish SMT (EUR/USD):**
- DXY makes lower high
- EUR/USD makes higher low (instead of lower low)
- EUR/USD is strong → Trade LONG on EUR/USD

**Note:** This is more advanced; start with positively correlated pairs

## How to Identify SMT Divergence

### Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Choose Your Correlated Pairs

**Recommended starting pairs:**
- **Forex:** EUR/USD & GBP/USD
- **Indices:** ES & NQ
- **Crypto:** BTC & ETH

**Pro tip:** Use pairs you already trade; don't add new instruments just for SMT

#### Step 2: Open Both Charts Side-by-Side

**Setup:**
- Split screen or dual monitors
- Same timeframe on both (15M, 5M, or 1M)
- Sync the charts to same time period

#### Step 3: Mark the Swing Points

**On both charts, mark:**
- Recent swing highs
- Recent swing lows
- Draw horizontal lines at these levels

#### Step 4: Wait for Divergence

**Watch for:**
- One chart makes new high/low
- Other chart FAILS to make new high/low
- This is your SMT divergence signal

#### Step 5: Confirm with Market Structure

**Check:**
- Higher timeframe bias (daily/4H)
- Is this at a premium/discount zone?
- Is there an Order Block nearby?
- Has liquidity been swept?

#### Step 6: Enter on the Weaker Asset

**Key rule:** Trade the asset that showed weakness (failed to make new high/low)

**Why?** This is where the reversal will be strongest

## Trading SMT Divergence (Step-by-Step)

### The Complete SMT Trade Setup

#### Phase 1: Pre-Trade Analysis

✅ **1. Identify Higher Timeframe Bias**
- Check daily or 4H chart
- Uptrend or downtrend?

✅ **2. Locate PD Array (Price Delivery Array)**
- Order Block
- Fair Value Gap
- Breaker Block
- Premium or Discount zone?

✅ **3. Mark Liquidity Pools**
- Equal highs/lows
- Previous day high/low

✅ **4. Time Check**
- Are we in a Kill Zone? (London/NY open)

#### Phase 2: Watch for Setup

✅ **5. Monitor Both Correlated Assets**
- Same timeframe (15M recommended)
- Watch as price approaches liquidity

✅ **6. Wait for Liquidity Sweep**
- Price takes out stops (equal highs or lows)

✅ **7. Identify SMT Divergence**
- One makes new high/low
- Other FAILS to make new high/low

#### Phase 3: Execution

✅ **8. Identify Order Block**
- On the WEAKER asset
- Where did reversal start after the divergence?

✅ **9. Wait for Price to Return to OB**
- Be patient
- Let price come to you

✅ **10. Enter the Trade**
- When price tests the Order Block
- Use 1M chart for precision entry

✅ **11. Set Stop Loss**
- Below OB (for longs)
- Above OB (for shorts)
- 10-20 pip buffer

✅ **12. Set Take Profit**
- Target 1: Opposing liquidity
- Target 2: Previous swing point
- Target 3: 2:1 or 3:1 RR

## Best Timeframes for SMT

### Higher Timeframe (4H, Daily)
- **Use for:** Overall bias and major turning points
- **Pros:** Very reliable, less noise
- **Cons:** Fewer setups, slower execution

### Mid Timeframe (15M, 1H) ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- **Use for:** Intraday trading and swing setups
- **Pros:** Good balance of accuracy and frequency
- **Cons:** Requires monitoring during Kill Zones

### Lower Timeframe (1M, 5M)
- **Use for:** Scalping and precise entries
- **Pros:** Many setups, quick profits
- **Cons:** More false signals, requires screen time

**Best practice:** Identify divergence on 15M, execute on 5M/1M

## Combining SMT with Other ICT Concepts

### SMT + Order Blocks (Essential Combo) ⭐⭐⭐

**Why it works:** SMT gives you the signal; OB gives you the entry

**Process:**
1. Identify SMT divergence
2. Find Order Block on weaker asset
3. Wait for price to test OB
4. Enter the trade

**This is the standard way to trade SMT**

### SMT + Liquidity Sweeps

**High-probability setup:**
1. Both assets approach liquidity (equal highs/lows)
2. Both sweep the liquidity
3. One makes new high/low, other doesn't (SMT)
4. Enter on weaker asset when it tests OB

**Why it works:** Liquidity sweep + SMT = strong institutional positioning

### SMT + Fair Value Gaps

**Setup:**
1. SMT divergence occurs
2. FVG is left on the weaker asset during reversal
3. Price returns to fill FVG
4. Enter when FVG is filled

**Bonus:** If FVG overlaps with OB = highest conviction trade

### SMT + Kill Zones

**Timing is critical:**
- **Asian Session:** Divergence less reliable (low volume)
- **London Open (2-5 AM):** Good for SMT setups
- **NY Open (7-10 AM):** BEST for SMT (highest volume)
- **London Close (10-12 PM):** Can work for reversals

**Rule:** Only trade SMT divergence during London or NY sessions

### SMT + Premium/Discount

**Confluence filter:**

**In Uptrend:**
- Wait for bullish SMT in discount zone (below 50%)
- Don't trade bullish SMT in premium zone

**In Downtrend:**
- Wait for bearish SMT in premium zone (above 50%)
- Don't trade bearish SMT in discount zone

**This adds another layer of probability**

## Advanced SMT Concepts

### 1. Multi-Asset SMT (3+ Pairs)

**Concept:** Monitor 3-4 correlated assets instead of just 2

**Example:**
- EUR/USD, GBP/USD, AUD/USD, NZD/USD (all USD pairs)
- If 3 make new highs but 1 doesn't → Strong SMT signal

**Why it works:** More confirmation = higher probability

### 2. SMT Divergence Strength

**Weak Divergence:**
- One asset makes slightly higher high/low
- Other makes slightly lower high/low
- Small difference

**Strong Divergence:**
- One asset makes significant new high/low
- Other fails dramatically
- Large difference = stronger signal

**Trade the strong divergences**

### 3. Hidden SMT

**What it is:** Divergence on lower timeframe but not visible on higher timeframe

**Example:**
- 15M chart: SMT divergence visible
- 1H chart: No divergence apparent

**Trading tip:** These are weaker signals; require more confirmation

### 4. SMT Clusters

**What it is:** Multiple SMT divergences across different timeframes

**Example:**
- 15M SMT divergence
- 1H SMT divergence
- 4H SMT divergence
- All aligning at same level

**Signal strength:** Extreme. These are rare but very powerful.

## Common SMT Mistakes

❌ **Mistake 1: Trading the wrong asset**
- Trading the stronger asset (the one making new high/low)
**Fix:** Always trade the WEAKER asset (the one that failed)

❌ **Mistake 2: Entering without Order Block**
- Entering based on SMT alone
**Fix:** SMT is confirmation, not entry. Find the OB first.

❌ **Mistake 3: Ignoring higher timeframe**
- Trading SMT against daily bias
**Fix:** Only trade SMT aligned with higher timeframe structure

❌ **Mistake 4: Trading weak correlations**
- Using pairs that don't correlate well
**Fix:** Stick to proven pairs (EUR/GBP, ES/NQ, BTC/ETH)

❌ **Mistake 5: Trading outside Kill Zones**
- Looking for SMT during Asian session or lunch
**Fix:** Only trade London/NY opens

❌ **Mistake 6: Over-complicating**
- Watching 10 pairs at once
**Fix:** Start with 2 pairs. Master them first.

❌ **Mistake 7: No liquidity sweep**
- Trading SMT without liquidity being taken
**Fix:** Wait for liquidity sweep BEFORE looking for SMT
