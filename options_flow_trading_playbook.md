## 3. Common Profitable Flow Patterns

### A. Opening Gap Flow
**Pattern**: Large pre-market or opening bell options activity
**Signal**: Institutional positioning for day's direction
**Action**: 
- Look for 5-10x average volume in first 30 minutes
- Confirm with price action breaking opening range
- Enter within first hour, target 1-3% move

### B. Reversal Flow
**Pattern**: Heavy put buying after extended rally OR call buying after selloff
**Signal**: Smart money positioning for trend change
**Action**:
- Wait for technical confirmation (break of trendline)
- Enter on pullback to key level
- Target previous swing high/low

### C. Breakout Confirmation Flow
**Pattern**: Options activity at technical breakout levels
**Signal**: Institutional conviction in breakout validity
**Action**:
- Confirm volume > 20-day average
- Enter on retest of breakout level
- Use options for leverage, target measured move

## 4. What Flow to Ignore

### Noise vs. Signal Filters:

| **IGNORE** | **WHY** | **HOW TO IDENTIFY** |
|------------|---------|---------------------|
| **Hedging Activity** | Already priced in, no directional edge | Multi-leg spreads, offsetting positions |
| **Rolling Positions** | Position maintenance, not new conviction | Same strike, different expiry |
| **Market Maker Activity** | Liquidity provision, not directional | Small size, both sides of book |
| **Retail Herding** | Follows price, doesn't lead it | Small lots, chasing moves |
| **Earnings Plays** | IV crush risk, already priced | Concentrated near earnings dates |

### Key Identification Rules:
1. **Single-leg > Multi-leg** for directional signals
2. **Ask execution > Bid execution** for bullish conviction
3. **OTM > ATM** for leverage and conviction signals
4. **New OI > Existing OI** for fresh positioning

## 6. Position Sizing Based on Flow Strength

### Flow Strength Matrix:

| **Strength** | **Criteria** | **Position Size** | **Risk per Trade** |
|--------------|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Strong** | >$1M premium, OTM, at ask, single-leg | 3-5% of portfolio | 1-1.5% of portfolio |
| **Medium** | $250K-$1M, technical confluence | 2-3% of portfolio | 0.5-1% of portfolio |
| **Weak** | <$250K, needs confirmation | 1-2% of portfolio | 0.25-0.5% of portfolio |
| **Very Weak** | Multi-leg, hedging likely | 0.5-1% or avoid | 0.1-0.25% of portfolio |

### Sizing Rules:
1. **Never risk >2%** on any single flow signal
2. **Scale in** on confirmation (50% initial, 50% on pullback)
3. **Reduce size** during high VIX (>30) or low liquidity
4. **Increase size** when flow aligns with multiple timeframes

## 7. Exit Strategies for Flow Trades

### A. Quick Scalp (Intraday)
- **Hold Time**: Minutes to 2 hours
- **Target**: 0.5-2% move in underlying
- **Stop**: Tight (0.25-0.5% below entry)
- **Best For**: High-conviction, large premium flows

### B. Swing Trade (1-5 days)
- **Hold Time**: 1-5 trading days
- **Target**: 2-5% move in underlying
- **Stop**: Technical level break
- **Best For**: Flow with technical confluence

### C. Position Trade (Weeks)
- **Hold Time**: 1-4 weeks
- **Target**: 5-10%+ move
- **Stop**: Major support/resistance break
- **Best For**: Structural flows with fundamental catalyst

### Exit Triggers:
1. **Price Target Hit**: Take 50% off at target 1, trail rest
2. **Time Decay**: Exit options with <7 days to expiry
3. **Flow Reversal**: Opposite flow appears at your strike
4. **Gamma Flip**: Dealer positioning changes (use GEX tools)
5. **Technical Failure**: Key level breaks against you

## 9. Edge Decay: Predictive Power Timeline

### Real-Time vs. Delayed:
- **0-2 minutes**: Maximum edge (90-100% predictive)
- **2-5 minutes**: Strong edge (75-90% predictive)
- **5-15 minutes**: Moderate edge (60-75% predictive)
- **15-60 minutes**: Weak edge (40-60% predictive)
- **1+ hours**: Noise (20-40% predictive)

### Why Edge Decays:
1. **Price moves** away from flow strike
2. **Dealer hedging** completes
3. **Follow-on flow** enters at worse prices
4. **News/events** change context
5. **Gamma exposure** shifts

### Actionable Insight:
- If you're >15 minutes late, wait for pullback
- After 1 hour, treat as informational only
- Old flow can indicate areas of interest but not timing

## 10. Combining Flow with Technicals

### A. Flow + Support/Resistance
**Method**: Look for flow at key technical levels
**Example**: Large call buying at 200-day MA during pullback
**Action**: Enter long on bounce, stop below MA
**Edge Increase**: +20-30% success probability

### B. Flow + Gamma Levels
**Method**: Use GEX to identify dealer pinning zones
**Example**: Positive GEX at $100, flow appears at $99
**Action**: Expect pinning toward $100, trade mean reversion
**Edge Increase**: +15-25% success probability

### C. Flow + Volume Profile
**Method**: Match flow with high-volume nodes
**Example**: Flow at Point of Control (POC)
**Action**: High-probability reversal at POC
**Edge Increase**: +10-20% success probability

### D. Flow + Market Structure
**Method**: Align flow with trend breaks/continuations
**Example**: Flow appears after breakout retest
**Action**: Enter in direction of breakout
**Edge Increase**: +25-35% success probability
